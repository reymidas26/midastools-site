## Short Film & Narrative

### 1. Opening Scene — Establishing Shot
```
A 8-second cinematic establishing shot of [LOCATION — e.g., "a rain-soaked city street at night" / "a vast desert landscape at golden hour" / "a quiet suburban neighborhood at dawn"]. Drone-style wide shot that slowly descends toward street level. Atmospheric lighting — [MOOD — e.g., "moody neon reflections in puddles" / "warm golden sun rays" / "misty blue pre-dawn light"]. Film grain. Anamorphic lens flare. 2.39:1 widescreen cinematic aspect ratio.
```

### 2. Character Introduction
```
A 6-second character introduction shot. [CHARACTER DESCRIPTION — e.g., "a woman in her 30s wearing a leather jacket"] walks into frame from the left. The camera is at eye level, static. The character pauses, looks directly at the camera for a beat, then continues walking past. Shallow depth of field — background is a soft blur. Side-lit with dramatic shadows. Film noir style. 2.39:1 widescreen.
```

### 3. Emotional Close-Up
```
A 5-second extreme close-up of a person's face showing [EMOTION — e.g., "quiet determination" / "overwhelming joy" / "deep contemplation" / "sudden realization"]. The camera is very close — filling the frame from forehead to chin. Natural, non-dramatic lighting from one side creates gentle shadows. The person's eyes tell the story — [DESCRIBE THE EYE MOVEMENT/EXPRESSION]. Subtle, restrained performance. 2.39:1 widescreen.
```

### 4. Chase/Action Sequence
```
A 10-second dynamic action sequence. [CHARACTER] running through [ENVIRONMENT — e.g., "a crowded marketplace" / "narrow alleyways" / "a train station"]. Handheld camera following from behind, creating urgency. Quick cuts between: feet hitting ground (2s), face looking back (2s), weaving through obstacles (3s), bursting through a doorway into light (3s). Desaturated color with high contrast. Motion blur on fast movements. 2.39:1 widescreen.
```

### 5. Flashback/Memory Sequence
```
A 8-second dream-like flashback sequence. [MEMORY SCENE — e.g., "two people laughing at a café table" / "a child running through a sunlit field"]. The footage has a warm, overexposed quality. Slightly slow motion (70% speed). Soft focus with light leaks. The edges of the frame are slightly vignetted and hazy. Nostalgic color grading — warm golds and faded greens. 2.39:1 widescreen.
```

## Trailer & Promo Styles

### 6. Product Launch Trailer
```
A 20-second product launch trailer. Beat 1 (0-3s): Black screen, single line of text fades in: "[TAGLINE]". Beat 2 (3-7s): Quick cuts of [YOUR PRODUCT] from different angles — each shot 1 second, with dramatic lighting changes. Beat 3 (7-12s): Product in use — [PERSON using it], reaction shot showing amazement. Beat 4 (12-17s): Feature montage — 3 key features shown in rapid succession. Beat 5 (17-20s): Logo reveal with release date. Cinematic sound design. 16:9 horizontal.
```

### 7. Event Teaser

Scene 2: Quick flashes of previous event highlights — crowd energy, speakers, networking. Scene 3: Date and location text appears with dramatic animation. Scene 4: "Register Now" call to action. Fast-paced editing. Energetic, exciting atmosphere. Concert/event lighting. 16:9 horizontal.


### 23. Zoom Through Effect

## Pro Tips for Cinematic Videos

1. **Aspect ratio = mood**: 2.39:1 = epic/cinematic, 16:9 = standard, 9:16 = intimate/mobile
2. **Camera movement tells the story** — push in = tension, pull back = reveal, static = contemplation
3. **Color grading is half the production** — specify: warm/cool, saturated/desaturated, contrast level
4. **Film grain adds character** — request "subtle film grain" for organic, non-digital feel
5. **Lighting vocabulary**: key light, fill light, backlight, rim light, motivated lighting
6. **Pacing**: fast cuts = energy/tension, long takes = contemplation/importance
7. **Sound design consideration** — design visuals that suggest audio (rain, footsteps, wind)

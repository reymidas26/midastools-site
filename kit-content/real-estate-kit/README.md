## How to Get the Best Results

### 1. Always Fill In the Variables

Every template uses [BRACKETS] to mark where you insert your own details. The more specific you are, the better the AI output.

**Weak input:**
> [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] = a house in Austin

**Strong input:**
> [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] = 4218 Ridgeview Trail, Austin, TX 78731

### 2. Set Your Agent Profile Once

Before your first session, paste this context block into your AI tool. It helps the AI match your voice and style for all future prompts:

```
You are helping me, a real estate agent, write professional communications.

My details:
- Name: [YOUR_NAME]
- Brokerage: [BROKERAGE_NAME]
- Market area: [CITY/REGION]
- Specialization: [e.g., luxury homes, first-time buyers, investment properties]
- Tone: [e.g., professional and warm, high-energy, consultative]
- License #: [YOUR_LICENSE_NUMBER]
- Phone: [YOUR_PHONE]
- Email: [YOUR_EMAIL]
- Website: [YOUR_WEBSITE]

Use this information whenever I ask you to write emails, texts, scripts, or marketing copy. Keep the tone consistent with my brand.
```

Save this as a note on your phone or a text file so you can paste it at the start of any new AI conversation.

### 3. Review Everything Before Sending

AI output is a first draft, not a final product. Always:

- Check facts and figures (AI can hallucinate MLS data)
- Verify property details match the actual listing
- Ensure compliance with your local real estate board rules
- Confirm pricing and dates are accurate
- Add your personal touch — a sentence about why you love the home, a local reference, something only a human would know

### 4. Build a Prompt Library

As you use these templates, you will develop favorites and variations. Save the ones that work best in a Google Doc, Notion page, or note app so you can reuse them instantly.

## Tips by AI Tool

### ChatGPT Tips
- Use "Custom Instructions" (Settings > Personalization) to save your agent profile permanently
- Create a "Real Estate" GPT with your profile baked in
- Use the mobile app for on-the-go lead responses

### Claude Tips
- Use "Projects" to save your agent profile as project context
- Claude handles long-form content well — ideal for listing descriptions and market reports
- Paste property details directly and ask for multiple variations at once

### Google Gemini Tips
- Gemini integrates with Gmail — use it to draft follow-up emails directly
- Use Google Docs integration for polished market reports
- Works well with Google Sheets for batch operations

### Microsoft Copilot Tips
- Use within Outlook for email follow-ups
- Integrates with Word for formal market reports
- Use Edge sidebar for quick access while browsing MLS

## Compliance Notes

- These prompts generate content that must be reviewed for compliance with your local MLS rules, NAR guidelines, and state real estate commission regulations.
- Never let AI generate specific legal or financial advice for clients.
- Always verify property data, pricing, and market statistics against actual MLS data.
- Fair Housing Act compliance is your responsibility. Review all generated content for any language that could be interpreted as discriminatory.
- Include required disclosures for your state/market in all client-facing communications.

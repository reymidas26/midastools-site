## 1. Full Resume Rewrite

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You have an existing resume that needs a complete overhaul.

```
Act as a professional resume writer with 15 years of experience placing candidates at top companies. Rewrite my resume to be modern, ATS-optimized, and compelling.

Here is my current resume:
[PASTE YOUR ENTIRE CURRENT RESUME]

Target role: [JOB TITLE]
Target industry: [INDUSTRY]
Years of experience: [NUMBER]

Rewrite this resume following these rules:
1. Use reverse chronological format
2. Start each bullet point with a strong action verb (never "Responsible for")
3. Include metrics and numbers wherever possible
4. Keep it to [1 or 2] page(s)
5. Use keywords from this target job description: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION OR KEY REQUIREMENTS]
6. Write a compelling professional summary (3-4 lines)
7. Organize skills into relevant categories
8. Remove outdated or irrelevant information

Format the output as a clean, structured resume I can copy into a document.
```

> **Pro Tip:** Always paste the target job description. A resume tailored to a specific role outperforms a generic resume by 3-5x in callback rates.

## 2. ATS Keyword Optimization

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You want to make sure your resume passes automated screening software.

```
You are an ATS (Applicant Tracking System) optimization expert. Analyze the following job description and my current resume, then help me optimize my resume to pass ATS screening.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION]

My Current Resume:
[PASTE YOUR RESUME]

Please:
1. Extract all important keywords and phrases from the job description (hard skills, soft skills, tools, certifications, industry terms)
2. Identify which keywords are MISSING from my resume
3. Identify which keywords I already have
4. Suggest exactly where and how to naturally incorporate the missing keywords
5. Rate my current ATS match score (estimated percentage)
6. Provide a revised version of my resume with the keywords integrated naturally — not stuffed awkwardly
7. Flag any formatting issues that might cause ATS parsing errors (tables, headers, graphics, columns)
```

> **Why This Works:** Most companies use ATS software that automatically rejects resumes missing key terms. This prompt ensures your resume speaks the same language as the job posting.

## 3. Bullet Point Generator

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You need to write strong experience bullet points from scratch.

```
I need powerful resume bullet points for my role as [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY NAME/TYPE]. I worked there from [START DATE] to [END DATE].

Here's what I did in this role (rough notes, doesn't need to be polished):
[DESCRIBE YOUR RESPONSIBILITIES AND ACCOMPLISHMENTS IN PLAIN LANGUAGE — BE AS SPECIFIC AS POSSIBLE]

Key metrics/numbers I can share:
- [ANY NUMBERS: team size, revenue, percentage improvements, number of projects, etc.]

Generate 8-10 resume bullet points following these rules:
1. Start each with a different strong action verb
2. Follow the formula: Action Verb + What You Did + Result/Impact
3. Include specific numbers wherever possible (even estimates are better than nothing)
4. Keep each bullet to 1-2 lines maximum
5. Prioritize accomplishments over responsibilities
6. Use industry-standard terminology for [INDUSTRY]
7. Make them ATS-friendly (no special characters or unusual formatting)

Then rank them from strongest to weakest so I can pick the top 4-5.
```

> **Pro Tip:** Even if you don't have exact numbers, use estimates. "Managed approximately 50 client accounts" is infinitely stronger than "Managed client accounts."

## 4. Achievement Quantification

**Level:** Mid / Senior / Executive
**When to use:** You know you did good work but struggle to put numbers on it.

```
I struggle to quantify my achievements. Help me turn vague accomplishments into metric-driven bullet points.

My role: [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Team size: [NUMBER]
Company size: [APPROXIMATE SIZE]

Here are my accomplishments described in plain language:

1. [VAGUE ACCOMPLISHMENT, e.g., "Improved the onboarding process"]
2. [VAGUE ACCOMPLISHMENT, e.g., "Helped increase sales"]
3. [VAGUE ACCOMPLISHMENT, e.g., "Made the website better"]
4. [VAGUE ACCOMPLISHMENT, e.g., "Led a big project"]
5. [VAGUE ACCOMPLISHMENT, e.g., "Trained new employees"]

For each one:
1. Ask me 2-3 clarifying questions that would help uncover the metrics (I'll answer, or tell me to estimate)
2. Then rewrite each as a quantified bullet point using the XYZ formula: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]"
3. If exact numbers aren't available, suggest reasonable estimate ranges based on industry benchmarks for a [COMPANY SIZE] company

Provide 2 versions of each: one conservative estimate and one that highlights the full scope.
```

> **Why This Works:** Hiring managers scan for numbers. Resumes with quantified achievements get 40% more interviews than those without. This prompt helps you find the metrics hiding in your experience.

## 5. Professional Summary Writer

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You need a compelling 3-4 line summary at the top of your resume.

```
Write a professional summary for the top of my resume. This should be 3-4 lines that immediately tell a hiring manager why I'm worth interviewing.

About me:
- Current/most recent title: [JOB TITLE]
- Years of experience: [NUMBER]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Top 3 skills: [SKILL 1], [SKILL 2], [SKILL 3]
- Biggest career achievement: [YOUR PROUDEST WIN]
- Target role: [WHAT YOU'RE APPLYING FOR]
- What makes me different: [YOUR UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION]

Rules:
1. No first person ("I am...") — use implied first person ("Results-driven...")
2. Include 2-3 keywords from this job description: [PASTE KEY REQUIREMENTS]
3. Lead with years of experience and core expertise
4. Include one specific metric or achievement
5. End with what I'm looking for or what I bring to the next role
6. Keep it under 4 lines / 60 words

Write 3 versions:
- Version A: Metric-focused (leads with numbers)
- Version B: Expertise-focused (leads with specialization)
- Version C: Impact-focused (leads with biggest achievement)
```

> **Pro Tip:** Your summary is the only section every recruiter reads. Spend extra time here. Test all 3 versions and pick the one that feels most authentic to you.

## 6. Skills Section Optimizer

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You need to organize your skills section for maximum ATS impact.

```
Help me build an optimized skills section for my resume.

My target role: [JOB TITLE]
My industry: [INDUSTRY]

Job description keywords and requirements:
[PASTE THE SKILLS/REQUIREMENTS SECTION OF THE JOB DESCRIPTION]

My actual skills (brain dump — include everything):
[LIST ALL YOUR SKILLS: technical, software, tools, methodologies, soft skills, languages, certifications]

Please:
1. Categorize my skills into 3-4 logical groups (e.g., "Technical Skills," "Tools & Software," "Methodologies," "Languages")
2. Prioritize skills that appear in the job description — put those first in each category
3. Remove any skills that are outdated or could hurt me (e.g., listing Windows XP in 2026)
4. Suggest 3-5 skills I should add based on the job description (only if I could reasonably claim them)
5. Format it as a clean, ATS-friendly skills section (no tables, no graphics, just categorized lists)
6. Flag any skills that are so basic they might make me look junior (e.g., "Microsoft Word," "Email")
```

> **Why This Works:** The skills section is the easiest place to win ATS keyword matches. A well-organized skills section can boost your ATS score by 20-30%.

## 7. Career Changer Resume

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You're pivoting to a new industry and need to reframe your experience.

```
I'm changing careers and need help reframing my experience for a completely new field.

My background:
- Previous industry: [OLD INDUSTRY]
- Previous role(s): [OLD JOB TITLE(S)]
- Years in previous field: [NUMBER]
- Key accomplishments: [LIST 3-5 ACCOMPLISHMENTS]

My target:
- New industry: [NEW INDUSTRY]
- Target role: [NEW JOB TITLE]
- Relevant training/certifications: [ANY NEW CREDENTIALS]
- Relevant side projects or volunteer work: [ANY RELEVANT EXPERIENCE]

Job description for target role:
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

Help me:
1. Identify 5-7 transferable skills from my old career that directly apply to the new role
2. Rewrite my top 5 bullet points from my previous experience, reframed using the language and priorities of [NEW INDUSTRY]
3. Write a professional summary that positions my career change as a strength, not a weakness
4. Suggest a resume format (chronological, functional, or combination) that best hides my lack of direct experience while highlighting transferable value
5. Create a "Relevant Skills" section that bridges both careers
6. Write a brief "Career Transition" statement I can include (optional section)
```

> **Pro Tip:** Career changers should lead with a combination or functional resume format that emphasizes skills over chronology. This prompt helps you make that pivot.

## 8. Entry-Level Resume

**Level:** Entry
**When to use:** You're a new graduate or have less than 2 years of experience.

```
I'm an entry-level candidate with limited work experience. Help me build a resume that competes with more experienced applicants.

About me:
- Degree: [DEGREE] from [UNIVERSITY], graduated [YEAR]
- GPA (if above 3.5): [GPA]
- Relevant coursework: [LIST RELEVANT COURSES]
- Internships: [DESCRIBE ANY INTERNSHIPS]
- Part-time/campus jobs: [LIST ANY WORK EXPERIENCE]
- Projects (class or personal): [DESCRIBE RELEVANT PROJECTS]
- Student organizations/leadership: [LIST CLUBS, ORGS, LEADERSHIP ROLES]
- Volunteer work: [LIST RELEVANT VOLUNTEER EXPERIENCE]
- Technical skills/tools: [LIST SKILLS]
- Certifications: [ANY CERTIFICATIONS, ONLINE COURSES, ETC.]

Target role: [JOB TITLE]
Target industry: [INDUSTRY]

Job description:
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

Help me:
1. Determine the best resume structure for someone with limited experience (education first vs. experience first)
2. Turn my coursework and projects into compelling bullet points that read like professional experience
3. Frame part-time jobs and campus roles as legitimate professional experience
4. Write a professional summary that emphasizes potential, education, and enthusiasm without sounding desperate
5. Create a "Projects" or "Relevant Experience" section that showcases real skills
6. Suggest what to include and what to leave off (don't pad — curate)
7. Keep it to one page, no exceptions
```

> **Why This Works:** Entry-level resumes fail when they try to look more experienced than they are. This prompt helps you own your level while maximizing the value of what you do have.

## 9. Executive Resume

**Level:** Executive
**When to use:** You're targeting VP, C-suite, or senior director roles.

```
I'm a senior executive and need a resume that positions me for [VP / SVP / C-SUITE / BOARD] roles.

My background:
- Current title: [TITLE]
- Years of leadership experience: [NUMBER]
- Industries: [INDUSTRIES]
- Company sizes managed: [REVENUE, HEADCOUNT, OR SCALE]
- P&L responsibility: [DOLLAR AMOUNT]
- Largest team managed: [NUMBER OF DIRECT + INDIRECT REPORTS]
- Board experience: [ANY BOARD ROLES]
- Key achievements:
  1. [MAJOR ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRICS]
  2. [MAJOR ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRICS]
  3. [MAJOR ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRICS]
  4. [MAJOR ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRICS]
  5. [MAJOR ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRICS]

Target role: [TARGET TITLE AND INDUSTRY]

Write an executive resume that:
1. Opens with a powerful executive summary (4-5 lines) that communicates my leadership brand
2. Includes a "Key Achievements" or "Career Highlights" section immediately after the summary with my 4-5 biggest wins (with metrics)
3. Frames each role in terms of SCOPE (budget, team, geography) and IMPACT (growth, transformation, efficiency)
4. Uses executive-level language (led, drove, transformed, spearheaded, orchestrated) without being cliche
5. Keeps the most recent 10-15 years detailed; earlier career in a brief "Earlier Career" section
6. Includes board roles, speaking, publications, and industry leadership if relevant
7. Is 2 pages maximum — executives don't need 5-page CVs
8. Reads like a CEO pitch, not a job application
```

> **Pro Tip:** Executive resumes are about narrative, not tasks. You're selling your ability to lead organizations, not complete assignments. This prompt ensures your resume reads like a leadership story.

## 10. Resume Gap Explainer

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You have employment gaps and need to address them without raising red flags.

```
I have a gap (or gaps) in my employment history and need help addressing this on my resume without lying or drawing unnecessary attention.

My gap details:
- Gap period: [START DATE] to [END DATE]
- Reason: [HONEST REASON — e.g., caregiving, health, layoff, travel, education, personal project, job market difficulty]
- What I did during the gap (if anything productive): [ANY COURSES, FREELANCE WORK, VOLUNTEERING, SKILLS LEARNED, PERSONAL PROJECTS]

My work history:
- Role before gap: [TITLE at COMPANY, DATES]
- Role after gap (or target role): [TITLE at COMPANY, DATES]

Help me:
1. Decide whether to address the gap directly on the resume or handle it in the cover letter/interview
2. If addressing on the resume, write a brief, professional entry for the gap period that's honest without over-explaining
3. Suggest formatting techniques that minimize the visual impact (years only vs. months, functional sections, etc.)
4. Write 2-3 sentences I can use in a cover letter to proactively address the gap
5. Prepare a confident 30-second verbal explanation for interviews
6. Highlight anything I did during the gap that adds professional value
```

> **Why This Works:** Gaps are only a problem when they look like you're hiding something. This prompt helps you address gaps with confidence rather than avoidance.

## 11. Action Verb Upgrader

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** Your bullet points all start with "Managed," "Helped," or "Responsible for."

```
Upgrade the action verbs in my resume bullet points. I want every bullet to start with a powerful, specific verb that conveys impact.

Here are my current bullet points (with their weak verbs):

1. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
2. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
3. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
4. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
5. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
6. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
7. [PASTE BULLET POINT]
8. [PASTE BULLET POINT]

My industry: [INDUSTRY]
My level: [ENTRY / MID / SENIOR / EXECUTIVE]

For each bullet:
1. Replace the opening verb with a stronger, more specific alternative
2. Ensure no two bullets in the same job section use the same verb
3. Match the verb intensity to my level (entry-level: "Assisted" is fine; executive: use "Orchestrated," "Spearheaded")
4. Keep the meaning accurate — don't exaggerate what I actually did
5. Provide 2 verb options for each so I can choose

Also provide a master list of 30 strong action verbs organized by category:
- Leadership & Strategy
- Achievement & Delivery
- Technical & Analytical
- Communication & Collaboration
- Efficiency & Optimization
```

> **Pro Tip:** Never start a bullet point with "Responsible for" — it describes a job description, not an accomplishment. Every bullet should prove you did something, not that something was assigned to you.

## 12. Resume Tailoring for Specific Jobs

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You have a solid base resume and want to quickly tailor it for a specific application.

```
I have a base resume and need to quickly tailor it for a specific job application. Don't rewrite the whole thing — just tell me exactly what to change.

My base resume:
[PASTE YOUR RESUME]

Target job description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION]

Company name: [COMPANY]
Company industry: [INDUSTRY]
Company size: [STARTUP / MID-SIZE / ENTERPRISE]

Provide a specific action plan:
1. Which keywords from the job description are missing from my resume? List each one with a suggested placement.
2. Which of my bullet points should I reorder or prioritize for this specific role?
3. Should I adjust my professional summary? If yes, provide the revised version.
4. Are there any bullets I should swap out or add specifically for this company?
5. Should I adjust my skills section? Provide the updated version.
6. Any other quick wins I can make in under 10 minutes to improve my match for this specific role?

Keep changes minimal and high-impact — I don't want to rewrite my entire resume for every application.
```

> **Why This Works:** Tailoring beats rewriting. This prompt gives you a surgical approach to customization that takes 10 minutes instead of 2 hours.

## 14. Freelance/Contract Work Formatter

- Key services provided: [WHAT YOU DID]
- Notable clients (if shareable): [CLIENT NAMES OR DESCRIPTIONS like "Fortune 500 retailer"]
- Best results/achievements:
  1. [ACHIEVEMENT]
  2. [ACHIEVEMENT]
  3. [ACHIEVEMENT]

Target role: [JOB TITLE — full-time position I'm applying for]

> **Why This Works:** Freelance work often looks scattered on a resume. This prompt consolidates it into a cohesive narrative that shows breadth of experience, not instability.

## 15. Resume Length Reducer

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** Your resume is too long and you need to cut it down.

```
My resume is currently [NUMBER] pages and needs to be [TARGET: 1 or 2] page(s). Help me cut it down without losing impact.

My resume:
[PASTE ENTIRE RESUME]

My target role: [JOB TITLE]
My years of experience: [NUMBER]

Help me:
1. Identify the weakest bullet points that can be cut entirely (explain why each is weak)
2. Identify bullets that can be combined into one stronger bullet
3. Flag any sections that are taking up too much space relative to their value
4. Suggest which roles can be condensed to 2-3 bullets (older or less relevant roles)
5. Identify any redundancies (skills listed in bullets AND skills section, etc.)
6. Trim my professional summary if it's too long
7. Provide the final, condensed version at [TARGET] page(s)

Rules:
- Keep my most impressive metrics and achievements
- Maintain ATS keyword density
- Don't cut anything that directly relates to [JOB TITLE]
- Cut from the bottom (oldest experience) first
```

> **Pro Tip:** The rule of thumb: 1 page for under 10 years of experience, 2 pages for 10+. Nobody reads page 3.

2. [PROJECT NAME]: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION, TECH STACK, YOUR ROLE, OUTCOME]
3. [PROJECT NAME]: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION, TECH STACK, YOUR ROLE, OUTCOME]

> **Why This Works:** Technical resumes fail when they're either too jargon-heavy for HR or too vague for the engineering manager. This prompt hits both audiences.

## 19. Resume Audit & Scoring

**Level:** All Levels
**When to use:** You have a resume draft and want an honest assessment before sending it out.

```
Act as a brutal but constructive resume reviewer. Score my resume and give me specific, actionable feedback.

My resume:
[PASTE ENTIRE RESUME]

Target role: [JOB TITLE]
Target company type: [STARTUP / MID-SIZE / ENTERPRISE / AGENCY]

Score my resume on a 1-10 scale for each category:
1. **First Impression** (Does the summary/header grab attention?)
2. **ATS Optimization** (Will this pass automated screening for the target role?)
3. **Achievement Focus** (Accomplishments vs. just listing responsibilities)
4. **Quantification** (How well are achievements backed by numbers?)
5. **Relevance** (How well does this match the target role?)
6. **Readability** (Clean formatting, appropriate length, easy to scan)
7. **Action Verbs** (Strong, varied, appropriate verbs)
8. **Keyword Density** (Relevant industry and role keywords)
9. **Story & Narrative** (Does this tell a compelling career story?)
10. **Overall Competitiveness** (Would this get an interview at a top company?)

For each score below 8, give me the specific fix — not just "improve this section" but the exact change to make.

End with: "Top 3 changes that would have the biggest impact on your interview rate."
```

> **Pro Tip:** It's uncomfortable to get your resume critiqued by AI. Do it anyway. The AI won't be as harsh as the recruiter who silently rejects you.

## Bonus: Resume Formatting Checklist

Before you submit any resume, run through this checklist:

- [ ] File format is .pdf (not .doc or .docx) unless specifically requested otherwise
- [ ] File name is professional: "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf"
- [ ] Font size is 10-12pt, consistent throughout
- [ ] Margins are 0.5-1 inch on all sides
- [ ] No tables, columns, headers/footers, or text boxes (ATS can't read these)
- [ ] No photos (unless required by country norm)
- [ ] No "References available upon request" (everyone knows this; it wastes space)
- [ ] All dates are consistent in format (month/year throughout)
- [ ] Contact info includes: name, phone, email, LinkedIn URL, city/state (no full address)
- [ ] Every bullet starts with an action verb
- [ ] No first-person pronouns ("I managed..." should be "Managed...")
- [ ] Spelling and grammar are perfect
- [ ] All links are working and professional

# 05 — Retention & Re-Engagement Emails

## 20+ AI Prompts for Win-Back Campaigns, Re-Engagement Sequences, Loyalty & Churn Prevention

Acquiring a new customer costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one. These prompts help you keep customers engaged, win back those who've gone quiet, reward loyalty, and prevent churn before it happens.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below, replace the `[PLACEHOLDER]` variables with your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice.

---

## Win-Back Campaigns

### Prompt 1 — "We Miss You" Win-Back Email

**When to use:** A customer or subscriber has been inactive for an extended period. This is the classic win-back email.

```
Write a win-back email for [BRAND NAME] to a [CUSTOMER/SUBSCRIBER] who has been inactive for [TIMEFRAME — e.g., "3 months", "6 months"].

Context:
- What they used to do: [PREVIOUS ACTIVITY — e.g., "They used to purchase monthly", "They opened every newsletter", "They were an active user of our platform"]
- What's changed since they left: [UPDATES — e.g., "We launched 3 new features", "We expanded our product line", "We improved our service based on feedback"]
- Win-back incentive: [OFFER — e.g., "20% off your next order", "One month free", "Exclusive access to our new [FEATURE]"]

The email should:
1. Acknowledge their absence without guilt-tripping: "It's been a while — and we've been busy making things better for you"
2. Share 2-3 updates that might re-spark their interest
3. Offer the incentive as a reason to come back NOW
4. Include a clear CTA: [CTA TEXT + URL]
5. Add a P.S. with a personal touch: [PS — e.g., "If there's a reason you left, I'd genuinely love to hear it. Just reply."]

Subject line: 5 options (avoid guilt — focus on what's new and what they're missing)
Tone: Warm, genuine, not desperate
Length: 150-200 words
```

### Prompt 2 — Win-Back Sequence: 3-Email Series

**When to use:** A strategic 3-email sequence to re-engage lapsed customers or subscribers.

```
Write a 3-email win-back sequence for [BRAND NAME] targeting [LAPSED SEGMENT — e.g., "customers who haven't purchased in 90+ days", "subscribers who haven't opened in 60 days"].

Known about this segment:
- Their last activity: [LAST ACTIVITY TYPE]
- Average previous engagement: [CONTEXT — e.g., "purchased 3 times over 6 months", "opened 80% of emails"]
- Likely reason for lapse: [HYPOTHESIS — e.g., "Found an alternative", "Life got busy", "Didn't see ongoing value"]

**Email 1 (Day 1) — The Friendly Check-In**
- Tone: Casual, personal, like a friend reaching out
- Content: "Hey, just checking in. We noticed you've been quiet and wanted to make sure everything's okay."
- Share one exciting update: [UPDATE]
- Soft CTA: "Come see what's new"
- No discount yet

**Email 2 (Day 4) — The Value Reminder**
- Tone: Value-focused, proof-driven
- Content: Remind them WHY they signed up/bought in the first place
- Include social proof: [TESTIMONIAL OR STAT — e.g., "Join 10,000+ [CUSTOMERS] who [BENEFIT]"]
- Share a customer success story relevant to them: [STORY NOTES]
- Medium CTA: [CTA]
- Still no discount

**Email 3 (Day 8) — The Incentive**
- Tone: Generous, final
- Content: "We want you back. Here's something special."
- Win-back offer: [OFFER + CODE]
- Deadline: [EXPIRY — e.g., "Expires in 72 hours"]
- Make it clear this is the last outreach: "This is the last time we'll reach out about this — but the door is always open."
- Strong CTA with urgency

Subject lines: 3 options per email
Each email: 100-180 words
```

### Prompt 3 — Subscription Cancellation Win-Back Email

**When to use:** Immediately after a customer cancels their subscription. Try to save them.

```
Write a subscription cancellation response email for [BRAND NAME].

Context: The customer just cancelled their [SUBSCRIPTION TYPE — e.g., "monthly plan", "annual membership", "premium subscription"].

Account details to reference:
- How long they were subscribed: [DURATION]
- Key usage: [WHAT THEY USED — e.g., "You created 47 campaigns during your subscription"]
- What they'll lose access to: [SPECIFIC FEATURES/BENEFITS]

Win-back options to present:
- Option A: [OPTION — e.g., "Pause for 1-3 months instead of cancelling"]
- Option B: [OPTION — e.g., "Downgrade to a lower tier at [PRICE]"]
- Option C: [OPTION — e.g., "Get [DISCOUNT] off your next [PERIOD] if you stay"]

The email should:
1. Acknowledge and respect their decision: "We're sorry to see you go"
2. Reference their specific usage (make them realize what they're giving up)
3. Present alternatives to full cancellation
4. Include a feedback question: "Would you mind sharing why you cancelled? [REASON OPTIONS — e.g., too expensive, not using enough, switched to competitor, missing feature]"
5. Leave the door open: "If you ever want to come back, your data will be here waiting."

Subject line: 3 options (respectful, not desperate)
Tone: Respectful, grateful for their time as a customer, no guilt
Length: 150-250 words
```

## Re-Engagement Emails

### Prompt 5 — Re-Engagement Email for Inactive Subscribers

**When to use:** Subscribers who are still on your list but haven't opened or clicked in a long time.

```
Write a re-engagement email for [BRAND NAME] targeting subscribers who haven't opened an email in [TIMEFRAME].

Goal: Get them to either re-engage or self-select off the list (a healthy list is better than a big one).

The email should:
1. Acknowledge the situation directly: "We've noticed our emails aren't landing with you lately"
2. Ask if they still want to hear from us: "Do you still want to receive [WHAT WE SEND]?"
3. Give them clear options:
   - Option A: "Yes, keep sending me emails" → [CTA — e.g., click to confirm]
   - Option B: "Send me emails less frequently" → [PREFERENCE CENTER LINK]
   - Option C: "No, please remove me" → [UNSUBSCRIBE LINK]
4. Remind them what they'll get if they stay: [VALUE PROP — 1 sentence]
5. Be transparent: "If we don't hear from you by [DATE], we'll remove you from our list to keep things tidy."

Subject line: 5 options (pattern interrupt — different from your normal emails)
Tone: Honest, respectful, no-pressure
Length: 80-150 words
```

## Churn Prevention Emails

### Prompt 16 — Usage Drop Warning Email

**When to use:** A user's engagement with your product has declined significantly. Intervene before they cancel.

```
Write a proactive churn prevention email for [PRODUCT NAME].

User context:
- Their usage pattern: [PREVIOUS USAGE — e.g., "Used to log in 5x/week, now logging in once every 2 weeks"]
- What they used most: [FEATURES — e.g., "The campaign builder and analytics dashboard"]
- Account status: [STATUS — e.g., "Active subscriber on [PLAN NAME]"]

The email should:
1. NOT say "we noticed you're using us less" (too surveillance-y)
2. Instead, lead with value: "Quick tip to get more out of [PRODUCT]"
3. Share a use case or tip related to what they used most: [TIP — e.g., "Did you know you can automate [TASK] with our [FEATURE]? Here's how in 2 minutes."]
4. Highlight a feature they haven't tried: [UNDERUSED FEATURE] — "Most users who try [FEATURE] see [BENEFIT]"
5. Offer 1:1 help: "Want a quick walkthrough? [BOOKING LINK] — 15 minutes and we'll optimize your setup."
6. Don't mention churn, leaving, or cancellation at all

Subject line: 3 options (value-driven, not alarming)
Tone: Helpful, proactive, like a friendly success manager
Length: 100-150 words
```

# 06 — Subject Lines, Preview Text, CTAs & Email Copywriting Frameworks

## 20+ AI Prompts for Subject Lines, Preview Text, Calls-to-Action, Email Copy Frameworks & A/B Testing

Your subject line determines whether your email gets opened or ignored. Your CTA determines whether readers take action. This file gives you prompts to generate high-converting subject lines, compelling preview text, powerful CTAs, and complete email copy using proven frameworks.

## Subject Line Generation

### Prompt 1 — Subject Line Generator (Universal)

**When to use:** Generate a batch of subject lines for any email. Your go-to prompt when you need options fast.

```
Generate 20 email subject lines for [BRAND NAME].

Email type: [TYPE — e.g., "promotional", "newsletter", "welcome", "cart abandonment", "product launch", "re-engagement"]
Email content summary: [1-2 SENTENCES describing the email's message]
Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
Tone: [TONE — e.g., "professional", "casual and fun", "urgent", "curiosity-driven", "friendly"]

Generate 20 subject lines using these categories:

**Curiosity (5 lines):**
- Make the reader NEED to know what's inside
- Use open loops, questions, or incomplete information

**Benefit-Driven (5 lines):**
- Lead with the outcome or value the reader gets
- Be specific about the benefit

**Urgency/Scarcity (5 lines):**
- Create FOMO or time pressure
- Use deadlines, limited quantities, or expiring offers

**Personal/Conversational (5 lines):**
- Sound like a text from a friend
- Use lowercase, first person, or questions

Rules for all subject lines:
- Keep under 50 characters when possible (mobile-friendly)
- No spam trigger words (FREE, ACT NOW, URGENT in all caps)
- No misleading clickbait that doesn't match the email content
- Include 2-3 with emojis and the rest without
- Vary the structure (questions, statements, lists, single words)
```

### Prompt 2 — Subject Line A/B Test Generator

**When to use:** Generate A/B test pairs to optimize your open rates.

```
Generate 10 A/B test pairs of email subject lines for [BRAND NAME].

For each pair, test ONE variable:

**Pair 1: Question vs. Statement**

What you're testing: Does a question or a statement drive more opens?

**Pair 2: With Emoji vs. Without Emoji**
A: [With emoji]
B: [Without emoji]
What you're testing: Do emojis increase or decrease open rate for this audience?

**Pair 3: Long vs. Short**
A: [Under 30 characters]
B: [40-60 characters]
What you're testing: Does brevity or detail win?

**Pair 4: Personalized vs. Generic**
A: [With [FIRST NAME] or personalization token]
B: [Without personalization]
What you're testing: Does personalization lift opens?

**Pair 5: Curiosity vs. Clarity**
A: [Vague/curiosity-driven]
B: [Clear/specific about contents]
What you're testing: Does mystery or transparency perform better?

**Pair 6: Number vs. No Number**
A: [Includes a specific number/stat]
B: [Same angle without the number]
What you're testing: Do numbers in subject lines boost opens?

**Pair 7: Fear of Missing Out vs. Positive Framing**
A: [FOMO/loss angle — "Don't miss...", "Before it's gone..."]
B: [Positive angle — "Here's your chance...", "You're invited..."]
What you're testing: Does loss aversion or positive framing win?

**Pair 8: First Person vs. Second Person**
A: [I/We language — "I just found..."]
B: [You language — "You need to see..."]
What you're testing: Does sender perspective or reader perspective drive more opens?

**Pair 9: Branded vs. Unbranded**
A: [Includes brand/product name]
B: [No brand name, just the hook]
What you're testing: Does brand recognition or curiosity win?

**Pair 10: Time-Specific vs. Evergreen**
A: [References today/this week/right now]
B: [No time reference]
What you're testing: Does time urgency increase open rates?

For each pair, also suggest:
- Which audience segment to test it on
- Minimum sample size recommendation
- What result would be statistically meaningful
```

## Call-to-Action (CTA) Optimization

### Prompt 7 — CTA Button Text Generator

**When to use:** Generate compelling CTA button text that drives clicks.

```
Generate 20 CTA button text options for an email from [BRAND NAME].

Desired action: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO — e.g., "buy the product", "book a call", "download the guide", "start a free trial", "read the blog post", "register for the webinar"]
Product/offer: [PRODUCT/OFFER DETAILS]
Audience: [AUDIENCE]

Generate CTAs in these categories:

**Action-Oriented (5 CTAs):**
- Start with a verb
- Examples: "Start My Free Trial", "Grab Your Discount"

**Benefit-Oriented (5 CTAs):**
- Focus on what they GET, not what they DO
- Examples: "Get Instant Access", "Unlock My Growth Plan"

**Urgency-Oriented (5 CTAs):**
- Create time pressure
- Examples: "Claim Before Midnight", "Save My Spot"

**Personal/First Person (5 CTAs):**
- Written from the reader's perspective
- Examples: "Yes, I Want This", "Count Me In"

Rules:
- 2-6 words per CTA (scannable)
- No generic "Click Here" or "Submit"
- Every CTA should make the reader think "yes, I want that"
- Match the tone of [BRAND NAME]: [TONE]
```

**Embedded in a sentence (5):**
Write a full sentence where the linked text feels natural.
Example: "You can see exactly how it works in our [free demo video]."

**P.S. line CTAs (5):**
Write P.S. lines with a built-in CTA.
Example: "P.S. If you only do one thing today, [check out our 2-minute product tour]. It'll change how you think about [TOPIC]."

Frame the CTA as a question that makes clicking feel like the obvious answer.
Example: "Ready to [get better results without working harder]?"

Rules:
- The linked text should be descriptive (not "click here")
- Each CTA should work as a standalone reason to click
- Vary the energy: some casual, some urgent, some curious
```

## Email Copywriting Frameworks

### Prompt 9 — PAS Framework Email (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**When to use:** The most versatile sales email framework. Works for any product or service.

```
Write an email using the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) framework for [BRAND NAME].

**Problem:** [THE PROBLEM YOUR AUDIENCE HAS — be specific]
Details: [CONTEXT — how this problem shows up in their daily life]

**Agitate:** [WHY THIS PROBLEM IS WORSE THAN THEY THINK]
Details: [CONSEQUENCES — what happens if they don't solve it? Cost, time wasted, missed opportunities, stress]

**Solve:** [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE AS THE SOLUTION]
Details:
- Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
- Key benefit: [#1 BENEFIT]
- Proof: [TESTIMONIAL, STAT, OR CASE STUDY]
- CTA: [CTA TEXT + URL]
- Price: [PRICE — if including]
- Offer: [SPECIAL OFFER — if applicable]

Framework guidelines:
- Problem section: 2-3 sentences. Make them nod and think "that's me"
- Agitate section: 3-4 sentences. Twist the knife (ethically). Make the cost of inaction clear
- Solve section: 3-5 sentences. Present the solution with proof and a clear CTA

Subject line: 5 options
Tone: [TONE]
Length: 200-350 words
```

### Prompt 10 — AIDA Framework Email (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

**When to use:** Classic marketing framework that builds momentum toward a conversion.

```
Write an email using the AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) framework for [BRAND NAME].

**Attention:** [THE HOOK — a surprising stat, bold claim, or pattern interrupt to open with]

**Interest:** [WHAT MAKES YOUR OFFER INTERESTING]
Details to include:
- What it is: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Who it's for: [AUDIENCE]
- What's unique: [DIFFERENTIATOR]

**Desire:** [WHAT CREATES DESIRE]
Elements to include:
- Emotional benefit: [HOW IT MAKES THEM FEEL]
- Practical benefit: [WHAT IT DOES FOR THEM]
- Social proof: [TESTIMONIAL OR STAT]
- Visual language: Paint a picture of life after using [PRODUCT]

**Action:** [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO]
- CTA: [CTA TEXT + URL]
- Urgency: [URGENCY ELEMENT]
- Risk reversal: [GUARANTEE OR ASSURANCE]

Framework guidelines:
- Attention: 1-2 sentences max. Stop the scroll.
- Interest: 2-3 sentences. Pull them in.
- Desire: 3-5 sentences. Make them WANT it.
- Action: 2-3 sentences. Make it easy and urgent.

Subject line: 5 options (should match the Attention hook)
Tone: [TONE]
Length: 200-350 words
```

**When to use:** Paint a transformation story. Especially effective for coaching, fitness, education, and SaaS.

```

**Before:** [THE READER'S CURRENT STATE]
Paint a vivid picture of their life/business/situation RIGHT NOW:
- What frustrates them: [FRUSTRATIONS]
- What they've tried: [FAILED ATTEMPTS]
- How it makes them feel: [EMOTIONS — e.g., overwhelmed, stuck, behind]

**After:** [THE READER'S DESIRED STATE]
Paint a vivid picture of life after using [PRODUCT/SERVICE]:
- What changes: [TRANSFORMATION]
- How it feels: [POSITIVE EMOTIONS]
- Specific outcomes: [MEASURABLE RESULTS]

**Bridge:** [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE — the thing that gets them from Before to After]
- Product: [PRODUCT NAME + BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- How it works: [2-3 SENTENCES]
- Proof it delivers: [TESTIMONIAL OR CASE STUDY]
- CTA: [CTA TEXT + URL]

Framework guidelines:
- Before: 3-5 sentences. Be specific enough that they feel seen.
- After: 3-5 sentences. Be specific enough that they can taste it.
- Bridge: 4-6 sentences. Connect the dots and give them a clear next step.

Subject line: 5 options
Tone: [TONE]
Length: 250-400 words
```

```

## Email Copy Components

### Prompt 14 — Email Opening Lines Generator

**When to use:** The first line of your email determines whether they keep reading. Generate killer openers.

```
Generate 20 opening lines for an email from [BRAND NAME] about [EMAIL TOPIC].

Audience: [AUDIENCE]
Email purpose: [PURPOSE]

Generate opening lines in these categories:

**Pattern Interrupts (4 lines):**
- Something unexpected that stops them mid-scroll
- Example: "I'm going to tell you something most [INDUSTRY] experts won't."

**Questions (4 lines):**
- Questions that make the reader think "yes" or "hmm, good question"
- Example: "When was the last time your [THING] actually worked the way you wanted?"

**Bold Claims (4 lines):**
- Start with a provocative statement backed by the email's content
- Example: "Most of what you've been told about [TOPIC] is wrong."

**Stats/Numbers (4 lines):**
- Open with a surprising data point
- Example: "78% of [AUDIENCE] make this mistake without realizing it."

**Story Hooks (4 lines):**
- Drop the reader into a moment
- Example: "Last Tuesday at 11:47 PM, I almost deleted everything."

For each, note: What emotion this opening creates (curiosity, surprise, recognition, urgency, intrigue)
```

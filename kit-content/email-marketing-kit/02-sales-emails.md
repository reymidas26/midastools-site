# 02 — Sales Emails, Product Launches & Promotional Campaigns

## 25+ AI Prompts for Sales Emails That Drive Revenue

This file covers every type of sales email you'll need — from product launches and flash sales to abandoned cart recovery and seasonal promotions. Each prompt is designed to generate conversion-focused copy that moves readers to action.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below, replace the `[PLACEHOLDER]` variables with your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice.

## Product Launch Emails

### Prompt 1 — Product Launch Announcement Email

**When to use:** You're launching a new product or service and need the main announcement email.

```
Write a product launch announcement email for [BRAND NAME].

Product details:
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- What it is: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- Who it's for: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
- Key benefits (top 3):
  1. [BENEFIT 1]
  2. [BENEFIT 2]
  3. [BENEFIT 3]
- Price: [PRICE]
- Launch offer: [SPECIAL OFFER — e.g., "20% off for the first 48 hours", "Free bonus included for early buyers", "Founding member pricing"]
- CTA: [CTA TEXT] linking to [CTA URL]

The email should:
1. Open with excitement — this is a big moment
2. Clearly explain what the product does and who it's for
3. Focus on benefits, not features
4. Include the launch offer with a deadline for urgency
5. Add social proof if available: [SOCIAL PROOF — e.g., beta tester quotes, waitlist size, endorsements]
6. One clear CTA (repeat it 2-3 times throughout the email)

Subject line: 5 options (mix of curiosity, benefit-driven, and urgency)
Preview text: 3 options
Tone: [TONE — e.g., excited but not overhyped, confident and direct]
Length: 250-400 words
```

### Prompt 2 — Pre-Launch Teaser Email

**When to use:** Build anticipation before a product launch. Send 3-7 days before the actual launch.

```

The email should:
1. Create curiosity without giving away the full product
2. Make the reader feel like an insider getting early access to information
3. Include a countdown element: "In [X] days, everything changes..."
4. CTA: [CTA — e.g., "Join the waitlist for early access", "Save your spot", "Get notified first"]
5. End with a P.S. that adds one more intriguing detail

Subject line: 5 options (pure curiosity — make them HAVE to open)
Tone: Mysterious, exciting, insider-feeling
Length: 100-200 words (shorter = more intrigue)
```

### Prompt 3 — Launch Sequence: Full 5-Email Series

**When to use:** Complete email sequence for a major product or course launch.

```
Write a 5-email launch sequence for [BRAND NAME]'s new [PRODUCT TYPE]: [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION — 2-3 sentences]
Price: [PRICE]
Target audience: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
Launch date: [DATE]
Cart close date: [DATE] (if applicable)
Launch offer: [OFFER]
Sales page: [URL]

Write all 5 emails:

**Email 1 — "The Announcement" (Launch Day)**
- Big reveal with full product details
- Focus on the transformation/outcome
- Launch offer with deadline
- Primary CTA to sales page

**Email 2 — "The Deep Dive" (Day 2)**
- Go deeper into what's included
- Feature-by-feature breakdown with benefit framing
- Address "Is this right for me?" with ideal customer description
- Include a testimonial or case study: [TESTIMONIAL]

**Email 3 — "The Social Proof" (Day 3)**
- Lead with results others have achieved
- Multiple testimonials or data points: [SOCIAL PROOF ELEMENTS]
- "Don't just take my word for it..." format
- CTA with urgency

**Email 4 — "The Objection Killer" (Day 5)**
- Address top 3 reasons people hesitate:
  1. [OBJECTION 1]
  2. [OBJECTION 2]
  3. [OBJECTION 3]
- Answer each directly and honestly
- Include guarantee details: [GUARANTEE]
- "If you're on the fence..." angle

**Email 5 — "Last Chance" (Final Day)**
- Cart is closing / offer is expiring
- Recap the offer and what they get
- Paint the "do nothing" vs "take action" contrast
- Final, urgent CTA
- P.S. with countdown or specific deadline

Each email: 200-350 words
Subject lines: 3 options per email
Tone: [TONE]
```

```

2. Explain why they're getting this (loyalty, engagement, being an early supporter)

4. Emphasize the exclusivity: [SCARCITY — e.g., "Only 50 spots at this price", "This email went to our top 500 subscribers"]

6. Ask them to keep it to themselves (creates more exclusivity, even if you don't enforce it)

Subject line: 5 options (VIP/exclusive feeling)

```

## Sales & Promotional Emails

```

- Who it's for: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
- The #1 problem it solves: [PROBLEM]

- What makes it different: [DIFFERENTIATOR]

The email should follow this structure:
1. **Hook**: Open with the reader's pain point or desired outcome
2. **Agitate**: Make the problem feel urgent (what happens if they don't solve it?)
3. **Solution**: Introduce the product as the answer
4. **Proof**: Include social proof: [SOCIAL PROOF]
5. **Offer**: State the price and what they get
6. **CTA**: Clear call-to-action: [CTA TEXT + URL]
7. **P.S.**: Add urgency, a bonus, or a guarantee: [PS CONTENT]

Subject line: 5 options
Tone: Confident, direct, not pushy
Length: 250-400 words
```

## Abandoned Cart & Recovery Emails

### Prompt 11 — Abandoned Cart Email #1 (Reminder)

**When to use:** First email after someone adds to cart but doesn't complete purchase. Send within 1-4 hours.

```
Write the first abandoned cart email for [BRAND NAME].

Cart contents: [PRODUCT NAME(S) AND PRICE]
Cart recovery link: [CTA URL]

The email should:
1. Be friendly, not pushy — they might have just gotten distracted
2. Remind them what's in their cart (include product name and image suggestion)
3. Make it easy to complete the purchase (one-click back to cart)
4. Do NOT offer a discount in this first email
5. Create subtle urgency: [URGENCY — e.g., "Items sell out fast", "Your cart is saved for 48 hours"]

Subject line: 5 options (casual, not desperate)
Tone: Helpful, light
Length: 50-100 words (brief — this is a reminder, not a sales pitch)
```

---

# 04 — Cold Outreach & B2B Emails

## 20+ AI Prompts for Cold Emails, B2B Outreach, Partnerships & Follow-Ups

Cold email is the highest-ROI sales channel when done right. These prompts help you write personalized, value-driven outreach that gets replies — not ignored. Every prompt follows proven cold email frameworks (PAS, AIDA, Before-After-Bridge) adapted for the AI era.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below, replace the `[PLACEHOLDER]` variables with your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice.

**Important:** Cold email effectiveness depends on relevance and personalization. The more specific your placeholders, the better your output.

## Cold Sales Emails

### Prompt 1 — Classic Cold Email (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**When to use:** Your bread-and-butter cold email. Use when reaching out to a prospect who hasn't heard of you.

```
Write a cold sales email using the Problem-Agitate-Solve framework.

Sender: [YOUR NAME], [YOUR TITLE] at [YOUR COMPANY]
Recipient: [PROSPECT NAME], [THEIR TITLE] at [THEIR COMPANY]
Industry: [THEIR INDUSTRY]

What we sell: [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE — 1 sentence]
The problem we solve: [SPECIFIC PROBLEM — e.g., "Most [THEIR ROLE]s spend 10+ hours/week on manual reporting"]
Why the problem is painful: [AGITATION — e.g., "That's 500+ hours a year — time that could be spent on strategy"]
Our solution in one sentence: [SOLUTION]
Relevant result: [PROOF — e.g., "We helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] reduce reporting time by 80% in 30 days"]

CTA: [DESIRED ACTION — e.g., "Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?", "Mind if I send a 2-minute demo video?", "Can I send over a case study?"]

Rules:
- Keep it under 100 words (short cold emails perform better)
- No fluff, no filler, no "I hope this email finds you well"
- One CTA only
- Make the first line about THEM, not you
- Sound human, not like a sales template

Subject line: 5 options (short, personalized, curiosity-driven)
Write 3 variations of this email with different angles.
```

```
- Under 100 words
```

### Prompt 5 — Value-First Cold Email (Give Before You Ask)

**When to use:** Instead of asking for a meeting, lead with free value — a tip, audit, or insight specific to them.

```
Details of the value:
1. [TIP/INSIGHT 1]
2. [TIP/INSIGHT 2]
3. [TIP/INSIGHT 3 — optional]

The soft pitch: [WHAT YOU DO — keep it to 1 sentence, positioned as "if you want help implementing this, we do exactly that"]

- Do not ask for a meeting in the first line
- The pitch should feel like a P.S., not the main event

Subject line: 3 options (hint at the value)
```

## B2B Outreach Sequences

### Prompt 7 — 4-Email Cold Outreach Sequence

**When to use:** A complete cold outreach campaign with strategic follow-ups.

```
Write a 4-email cold outreach sequence for [YOUR COMPANY] targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE — e.g., "VP of Marketing at B2B SaaS companies with 50-200 employees"].

What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
Main value prop: [VALUE PROPOSITION]
Key proof point: [CASE STUDY OR STAT]
CTA goal: [DESIRED ACTION — e.g., book a demo, schedule a call]

Write all 4 emails:

**Email 1 (Day 1) — The Opener**
- Lead with their problem or a relevant observation
- Brief intro of what you do
- Light CTA (low commitment)
- Under 80 words

**Email 2 (Day 3) — The Value Add**
- Don't repeat Email 1
- Share a relevant resource, insight, or case study
- Frame it as genuinely useful, not just a follow-up excuse
- Reference a result: [PROOF POINT]
- Under 60 words

**Email 3 (Day 7) — The Social Proof**
- Lead with a testimonial or case study
- "[SIMILAR COMPANY] was in the same situation — here's what happened..."
- Connect to the prospect's situation
- CTA with slightly more urgency
- Under 80 words

**Email 4 (Day 14) — The Breakup**
- Final follow-up
- Brief, respectful: "I don't want to keep bothering you"
- Restate the core value in one sentence
- Leave the door open: "If the timing's ever right, [CTA]"
- Under 50 words

Subject lines for each email (3 options per email)
Tone: Professional, confident, never desperate
```

## Follow-Up Emails

### Prompt 13 — First Follow-Up (No Response)

**When to use:** They didn't reply to your initial email. Send 2-3 days later.

```
Write a follow-up email to a cold prospect who didn't respond to my initial outreach.

Original email context: [BRIEF SUMMARY — e.g., "I reached out about helping them with [SERVICE] and shared a case study"]

The follow-up should:
1. NOT just say "Bumping this to the top of your inbox" or "Just following up"
2. Add NEW value — one of these approaches:
   - [OPTION A] Share a relevant resource: [RESOURCE]
   - [OPTION B] Share a new proof point: [PROOF]
   - [OPTION C] Ask a thought-provoking question: [QUESTION]
   - [OPTION D] Reference something new about their company: [OBSERVATION]
3. Briefly reconnect to the original email (1 sentence)
4. New CTA (same ask, different framing)
5. Under 60 words

Subject line: Reply to original thread OR 3 new options
Tone: Helpful, not desperate
```

---

### Prompt 14 — Second Follow-Up (Still No Response)

**When to use:** Third email in the sequence. Different angle, more direct.

```
Write a second follow-up email to a prospect who hasn't responded to 2 previous emails.

Previous outreach summary: [BRIEF CONTEXT]

Approach: Choose one of these strategies:
- **The Quick Question**: Ask a single, easy-to-answer yes/no question
- **The Relevant News**: Reference industry news that connects to your offer
- **The Case Study Drop**: Share a 2-sentence case study result
- **The "Wrong Person?"**: Ask if there's someone else you should be talking to

Selected approach: [WHICH ONE]
Details: [RELEVANT DETAILS FOR CHOSEN APPROACH]

Rules:
- Under 40 words
- No guilt-tripping
- Still professional and respectful
- Make it effortless to reply (yes/no question or low-friction CTA)

Subject line: 3 options (or reply to thread)
```


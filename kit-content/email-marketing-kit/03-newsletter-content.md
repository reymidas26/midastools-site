# 03 — Newsletter Content & Recurring Email Templates

## 20+ AI Prompts for Newsletters, Content Roundups & Thought Leadership Emails

Newsletters are the backbone of email marketing — they keep your audience engaged week after week. But coming up with fresh content every send is exhausting. These prompts make it effortless to generate newsletter content that your subscribers actually look forward to reading.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below, replace the `[PLACEHOLDER]` variables with your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice.

---

## Weekly Newsletter Templates

### Prompt 1 — Standard Weekly Newsletter

**When to use:** Your go-to template for a reliable weekly newsletter. Works for any industry.

```
Write a weekly newsletter issue for [NEWSLETTER NAME] by [BRAND/AUTHOR NAME].

Newsletter topic area: [TOPIC — e.g., "digital marketing", "personal finance", "startup growth", "AI tools"]
Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
Issue number: [NUMBER] (if applicable)
Send date: [DATE]

This week's content:

**Main story/insight:**
[TOPIC OF MAIN SECTION — e.g., "Why most founders are measuring the wrong metrics" or "3 underrated AI tools I discovered this week"]
Key points to cover: [NOTES/BULLET POINTS]

**Secondary items (brief takes on 2-3 topics):**
1. [TOPIC 1]: [YOUR TAKE/NOTES]
2. [TOPIC 2]: [YOUR TAKE/NOTES]
3. [TOPIC 3]: [YOUR TAKE/NOTES]

**Resource/recommendation of the week:**
[RESOURCE — e.g., article, tool, book, podcast episode]
Why it's worth checking out: [REASON]

**CTA/engagement prompt:**
[CTA — e.g., "Reply with your answer", "Check out our latest product", "Share this with a friend"]

Format guidelines:
- Use headers and short paragraphs for scannability
- Main section: 200-300 words
- Secondary items: 50-80 words each
- Conversational tone throughout
- Include a brief personal note or greeting at the top

Subject line: 5 options
Total length: 400-600 words
Tone: [TONE — e.g., "smart friend sharing insights over coffee"]
```

```
Evidence/examples:
1. [EVIDENCE 1 — stat, case study, personal experience]
2. [EVIDENCE 2]
3. [EVIDENCE 3]

What the reader should do instead: [ACTIONABLE ALTERNATIVE]

The email should:
1. Open with the myth stated confidently — then immediately challenge it
2. Explain why the myth persists (shows empathy, not arrogance)
3. Present your counter-argument with evidence
4. Give the reader a clear alternative action
5. Invite debate: "Agree? Disagree? Reply and tell me why."

Subject line: 5 options (provocative, challenge assumptions)
Tone: Confident, evidence-based, not preachy
Length: 400-600 words
```

# 01 — Welcome Sequences & Onboarding Emails

## 25+ AI Prompts for Welcome Emails, Onboarding Sequences & New Subscriber Nurturing

Welcome emails get 4x more opens and 5x more clicks than regular marketing emails. This file gives you complete, ready-to-use prompts to create welcome sequences that turn new subscribers into loyal customers.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below, replace the `[PLACEHOLDER]` variables with your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice.

## Single Welcome Emails

### Prompt 1 — Classic Welcome Email (Any Business)

**When to use:** The very first email a new subscriber receives. Works for any industry.

```
Write a welcome email for [BRAND NAME], a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] that helps [TARGET AUDIENCE] achieve [MAIN BENEFIT].

The subscriber just signed up via [SIGNUP SOURCE — e.g., homepage opt-in, lead magnet download, webinar registration].

The email should:
- Open with a warm, personal greeting
- Confirm what they signed up for
- Set expectations for what emails they'll receive and how often
- Include one clear call-to-action: [CTA — e.g., "Download your free guide", "Browse our top products", "Book a discovery call"]
- Close with a personal sign-off from [SENDER NAME], [SENDER TITLE]

Tone: [TONE — e.g., friendly and approachable, professional and authoritative, casual and fun]
Length: 150-250 words
Include a subject line and preview text.
```

## Multi-Email Welcome Sequences

### Prompt 6 — 5-Email Welcome Sequence (Service Business)

**When to use:** Create a complete 5-email onboarding sequence for a service-based business (agency, consulting, coaching, freelancer).

```
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for [BRAND NAME], a [TYPE OF SERVICE BUSINESS] that helps [TARGET AUDIENCE] [ACHIEVE WHAT].

Goal of the sequence: Nurture the new subscriber from "just browsing" to "ready to book a [DESIRED ACTION — e.g., discovery call, consultation, demo]."

Subscriber context: They signed up via [SIGNUP SOURCE] and their primary pain point is [PAIN POINT].

Write all 5 emails with subject lines, preview text, and body copy:

**Email 1 (Day 0) — Welcome & Deliver Value**
- Deliver the promised [LEAD MAGNET/CONTENT]
- Set expectations for the sequence
- Introduce yourself/brand briefly

**Email 2 (Day 2) — The Problem**
- Agitate the core pain point: [PAIN POINT DETAILS]
- Share a relatable story or scenario
- Position your expertise (don't sell yet)

**Email 3 (Day 4) — The Solution**
- Introduce your methodology/approach: [YOUR APPROACH]
- Share a case study or result: [CASE STUDY — e.g., "Helped [CLIENT TYPE] achieve [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]"]
- Include social proof

**Email 4 (Day 6) — Objection Handling**
- Address the top 3 objections:
  1. [OBJECTION 1 — e.g., "It's too expensive"]
  2. [OBJECTION 2 — e.g., "I've tried this before and it didn't work"]
  3. [OBJECTION 3 — e.g., "I don't have enough time"]
- Use testimonials or data to counter each

**Email 5 (Day 8) — The Ask**
- Make the direct offer to [DESIRED ACTION]
- Recap the value and transformation
- Add urgency if possible: [URGENCY ELEMENT — e.g., "Limited spots this month", "Price increases on [DATE]"]
- Strong CTA

Tone: [TONE]
Each email: 150-250 words
```

**When to use:** A personal-feeling check-in from the founder or customer success lead during onboarding.

```

- What they signed up for: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]

1. Open casually: "Hey [FIRST NAME], just wanted to check in..."

Tone: Genuine, human, casual
Subject line: Should look like a personal email (no marketing language)
Length: 80-120 words (short = more believable as personal)
```

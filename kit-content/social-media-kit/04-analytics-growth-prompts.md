## SECTION 1: AUDIENCE ANALYSIS

### 1. Deep Audience Persona Builder

**Platform:** All platforms
**Best for:** Understanding exactly who your audience is and what they want

```
I run a social media account for [BUSINESS/NICHE] on [PLATFORMS]. Here's what I know about my current audience:

- Platform demographics: [AGE RANGE, GENDER SPLIT, TOP LOCATIONS — from your analytics]
- Follower count: [NUMBER]
- Average engagement rate: [X%]
- Top performing content topics: [LIST 3-5]
- Most active times: [FROM YOUR ANALYTICS]

Build 3 detailed audience personas for my social media:

For each persona:
1. Name and demographic profile (age, location, job, income level)
2. Social media behavior:
   - Which platforms they use most and when
   - How they discover new accounts (explore, hashtags, shares, search)
   - What makes them follow vs. scroll past
   - What makes them engage (like, comment, save, share)
   - What makes them unfollow
3. Content preferences:
   - Topics they care about most
   - Content formats they prefer (video, carousel, text, stories)
   - Tone they respond to (professional, casual, funny, inspirational)
   - Length preference (short-form vs. long-form)
4. Pain points (3-5 specific problems my content should address)
5. Goals (3-5 things they're trying to achieve)
6. Objections (why they might NOT buy from me)
7. Trigger words and phrases that resonate with them
8. Where else they spend time online (subreddits, Discord servers, podcasts, newsletters)

Then create a "Content Compass" — a one-page guide showing:
- Topics to cover most often (based on persona overlap)
- Content formats to prioritize
- Tone and voice guidelines
- Posting schedule optimized for when these personas are online
```

**Pro Tips:**
- Update these personas quarterly as your audience evolves
- Create separate content strategies for each persona if they differ significantly
- Use platform analytics as the starting point, not just guesses

## SECTION 2: COMPETITOR TRACKING

### 4. Competitor Analysis Framework

**Platform:** All platforms
**Best for:** Understanding what's working for competitors and finding gaps

```
I want to analyze my competitors on [PLATFORM] in the [NICHE] space.

My competitors are:
1. [COMPETITOR 1 — handle, follower count, brief description]
2. [COMPETITOR 2 — handle, follower count, brief description]
3. [COMPETITOR 3 — handle, follower count, brief description]

For each competitor, analyze:

1. Content Strategy:
   - What content types do they post most? (video, carousel, text, stories)
   - What topics do they cover?
   - What's their posting frequency?
   - What's their content mix? (educational vs. entertaining vs. promotional)

2. Engagement Patterns:
   - Which of their posts get the most engagement?
   - What hooks do they use?
   - How do they drive comments? (questions, controversies, polls)
   - What's their reply strategy? (Do they respond to comments?)

3. Strengths & Weaknesses:
   - What are they doing really well?
   - What gaps exist in their content? (What do they NOT cover?)
   - What complaints do their followers have? (Check comments)

4. Opportunities for Me:
   - Topics they cover poorly or not at all
   - Content formats they don't use
   - Audience segments they're ignoring
   - Ways I can differentiate my content

5. Competitive Content Calendar:
   - Based on this analysis, create 10 content ideas that position me differently from all 3 competitors
   - Each idea should fill a gap or improve on something a competitor does

My current content strategy: [DESCRIBE BRIEFLY]
My unique angle or expertise: [DESCRIBE]
```

## SECTION 5: A/B TESTING

### 10. Social Media A/B Test Framework

**Platform:** All platforms
**Best for:** Systematically testing content to find what works best

```
I want to run A/B tests on my social media content on [PLATFORM] to improve [METRIC — engagement rate, reach, follower growth, saves, comments].

Create a complete A/B testing framework:

1. WHAT TO TEST (prioritized list of 10 variables):
   - Hook styles (question vs. statement vs. statistic)
   - Caption length (short vs. long)
   - Posting time (morning vs. evening)
   - Content format (carousel vs. single image vs. reel)
   - CTA type (question vs. save prompt vs. share prompt)
   - Hashtag count and type
   - First slide/thumbnail design
   - Tone (professional vs. casual)
   - Post frequency (daily vs. 3x/week)
   - Visual style (minimal vs. bold)

2. TEST STRUCTURE for each variable:
   - Hypothesis (what I think will win and why)
   - Test setup (how to create comparable A/B versions)
   - Duration (how many posts to test before declaring a winner)
   - Metrics to track
   - How to control for other variables

3. FIRST TEST to run this week:
   - Variable: [RECOMMENDED BASED ON MY SITUATION]
   - Version A: [SPECIFIC CONTENT]
   - Version B: [SPECIFIC CONTENT]
   - When to post each version
   - How to measure results
   - What "winning" looks like

4. TESTING CALENDAR:
   - Month 1: Test [VARIABLE]
   - Month 2: Test [VARIABLE]
   - Month 3: Test [VARIABLE]
   - Compounding strategy (how each test builds on the last)

My current content approach: [DESCRIBE]
My biggest challenge: [DESCRIBE]
My goal: [WHAT I WANT TO IMPROVE]
```

## SECTION 1: RESPONSE TEMPLATES

### 1. Complete Comment Response Library

**Platform:** All platforms
**Best for:** Responding to every type of comment quickly and on-brand

```
I manage social media for [BUSINESS/BRAND] in the [NICHE] space. My brand voice is [DESCRIBE — e.g., warm and helpful, witty and direct, professional and empathetic].

Create a comment response library with 3 response options for each scenario:

POSITIVE COMMENTS:
1. "Love this!" / Generic praise → 3 responses that deepen the conversation
2. "This helped me so much" / Specific praise → 3 responses that ask for more detail
3. "Just bought it!" / Purchase celebration → 3 responses that make them feel valued
4. Tagging a friend → 3 responses that welcome the tagged person
5. Sharing their own success story → 3 responses that amplify their story

QUESTIONS:
6. Product/service question → 3 responses that answer + soft-sell
7. Pricing question → 3 responses that handle price gracefully
8. "How do I get started?" → 3 responses with clear next steps
9. Off-topic question → 3 responses that redirect politely
10. Question you don't know the answer to → 3 responses that are honest and helpful

NEGATIVE COMMENTS:
11. Constructive criticism → 3 responses that show you listen
12. Customer complaint → 3 responses that move to DM
13. "Too expensive" → 3 responses that reframe value
14. Competitor comparison ("X is better") → 3 responses that stay classy
15. Trolling/hate → 3 responses (when to respond, when to ignore, when to delete)

ENGAGEMENT OPPORTUNITIES:
16. Someone shares a relevant experience → 3 responses that start a conversation
17. Industry debate in comments → 3 responses that position you as an authority
18. "Can I DM you?" → 3 responses that open the door
19. Influencer or brand comments → 3 responses that build the relationship
20. Comment from a potential lead → 3 responses that move toward conversion

For each response:
- Keep it under 2-3 sentences
- Match my brand voice
- Include a follow-up question or CTA where natural
- Note when to take the conversation to DMs
```

**Pro Tips:**
- Respond within 1 hour of comments on new posts — the algorithm rewards active conversations
- Never copy-paste the exact same response twice — rotate between your 3 options
- When in doubt, ask a question back — it extends the conversation and boosts engagement

## SECTION 2: DM SEQUENCES

### 3. Sales-Focused DM Sequences

**Platform:** Instagram, LinkedIn, X/Twitter
**Best for:** Turning followers into customers through direct messages

```
I sell [PRODUCT/SERVICE] at [PRICE POINT] through social media. My audience is [DESCRIBE].

Create 3 DM sequences for different scenarios:

SEQUENCE 1: WARM LEAD (someone who engaged with my content)
- DM 1 (Day 1): Thank them for engaging + start conversation
- DM 2 (Day 2): Provide unexpected value (tip, resource, insight)
- DM 3 (Day 4): Ask about their current situation/challenge
- DM 4 (Day 6): Share a relevant success story or case study
- DM 5 (Day 8): Soft pitch with a specific offer or next step

SEQUENCE 2: INBOUND INQUIRY (someone who DMs asking about your product/service)
- DM 1: Acknowledge + ask 1-2 qualifying questions
- DM 2: Answer based on their response + share social proof
- DM 3: Present the offer with clear next steps
- DM 4 (if no response after 48hrs): Gentle follow-up
- DM 5 (if still no response after 5 days): Final touchpoint

SEQUENCE 3: COLD OUTREACH (reaching out to potential customers/collaborators)
- DM 1: Personalized opener referencing their content + genuine compliment
- DM 2 (only if they respond): Continue conversation, provide value
- DM 3: Share something helpful (not a pitch)
- DM 4: Mention how you might help (if conversation naturally goes there)
- DM 5: Direct but low-pressure offer

For each DM:
- Write the exact message text
- Note the tone (casual, professional, friendly)
- Include emoji usage guidelines
- Note what to do if they don't respond
- Note what to do if they respond negatively
- Include "if/then" branches (if they say X, respond with Y)

Rules:
- Never spam
- Always provide value before asking for anything
- Keep messages short (2-4 sentences max)
- Sound human, not scripted
- Include opt-out: "No worries if not interested, just thought of you!"
```

### 4. Welcome DM Sequence for New Followers

**Platform:** Instagram, X/Twitter
**Best for:** Making a great first impression with new followers

```
I want to welcome new followers with a DM that provides immediate value and starts a relationship.

My account: [DESCRIBE — niche, follower count, what I sell]
My brand voice: [DESCRIBE]

Create a new follower welcome system:

1. WELCOME DM (sent within 24 hours of follow):
   - 3 versions to rotate between
   - Each should: thank them, provide immediate value, ask one question
   - Include a free resource link if applicable
   - Keep under 4 sentences

2. FOLLOW-UP DM (sent 3 days after welcome, only if they responded):
   - 3 versions based on common responses
   - Continue the conversation naturally
   - Offer to help with something specific

3. VALUE DM (sent 7 days after follow):
   - Share a tip, resource, or insight
   - No pitch — pure value
   - Ask what they're working on

4. SEGMENTATION QUESTION (sent 14 days after follow):
   - Ask what type of content they want most from you
   - Use their answer to customize future content
   - Make them feel heard

Rules:
- If someone doesn't respond to DM 1, don't send more DMs
- Keep everything casual and human — no sales speak
- Make it easy to respond (ask yes/no or "A or B" questions)
- Mention you're a real person, not a bot
```

## SECTION 3: CRISIS MANAGEMENT

### 6. Social Media Crisis Playbook

**Platform:** All platforms
**Best for:** Handling PR issues, backlash, and emergencies on social media

```
I manage social media for [BUSINESS/BRAND]. I need a crisis management playbook for social media.

Create a comprehensive crisis response plan:

1. CRISIS SEVERITY LEVELS:
   - Level 1 (Minor): Negative comment, minor complaint, small misunderstanding
   - Level 2 (Moderate): Multiple complaints, trending negative sentiment, bad review going viral
   - Level 3 (Major): Full-blown PR crisis, brand boycott threat, legal/safety issue

2. For each level, provide:
   - Response time target (how fast to respond)
   - Who should be involved
   - Communication channels to use
   - Template response (first response)
   - Follow-up response (24 hours later)
   - Resolution response (when issue is fixed)

3. SPECIFIC CRISIS SCENARIOS AND RESPONSES:
   a. Product/service failure complaint going viral
      - Initial acknowledgment post
      - DM template to affected customers
      - Public update post (with solution)

   b. Employee or founder says something controversial
      - Assessment framework (is an apology needed?)
      - Apology template (if needed)
      - Non-apology acknowledgment (if blown out of proportion)

   c. Negative review or attack from an influencer
      - Response strategy
      - When to respond publicly vs. privately
      - When to ignore completely

   d. Data breach or security issue
      - Immediate notification template
      - FAQ response template
      - Follow-up communication plan

   e. Insensitive post accidentally published
      - Takedown decision framework
      - Apology template
      - Recovery content plan

4. CRISIS PREVENTION CHECKLIST:
   - Pre-publishing content review process
   - Sensitive topic guidelines
   - Approval chain for controversial content
   - Social media policy for team members

5. POST-CRISIS RECOVERY PLAN:
   - When to resume normal posting
   - First 5 posts after a crisis
   - How to rebuild trust and credibility

My industry: [INDUSTRY]
My audience sensitivity level: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]
My team size: [NUMBER]
```

# 05 — Training & Workshop Deck Prompts

## 20+ AI Prompts for Training Presentations That Actually Teach

These prompts help you create training presentations, workshop materials, onboarding decks, and educational content. Each prompt generates structured learning content with clear objectives, engaging activities, and practical takeaways that participants can apply immediately.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

## Employee Onboarding Presentations

### Prompt 1 — New Employee Onboarding Presentation

Use this to create a comprehensive onboarding deck for new hires that makes them feel welcome and sets them up for success.

```
You are a people operations expert who designs world-class employee onboarding experiences. Create an onboarding presentation for new hires.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Company size: [NUMBER OF EMPLOYEES]
Role being onboarded: [SPECIFIC ROLE — OR "GENERAL / ALL NEW HIRES"]
Onboarding format: [IN-PERSON / VIRTUAL / HYBRID]
Onboarding duration for this session: [1 HOUR / HALF DAY / FULL DAY / MULTI-DAY]

Company details:
- Mission: [COMPANY MISSION]
- Values: [LIST COMPANY VALUES]
- Founded: [YEAR]
- Key products/services: [WHAT THE COMPANY DOES]
- Customers: [WHO YOU SERVE]
- Culture: [DESCRIBE THE CULTURE IN 2-3 SENTENCES]
- Leadership team: [KEY LEADERS AND THEIR ROLES]
- Office/remote policy: [WORK ARRANGEMENT]

What new hires need to know:
- Systems/tools to set up: [LIST TOOLS — e.g., Slack, Jira, GSuite, Salesforce]
- Key processes: [IMPORTANT PROCESSES — e.g., how to submit expenses, request PTO, get IT help]
- First week expectations: [WHAT THEY SHOULD ACCOMPLISH IN WEEK 1]
- First 30-day goals: [WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE IN THE FIRST MONTH]
- Key contacts: [WHO TO GO TO FOR WHAT]
- Benefits overview: [KEY BENEFITS TO HIGHLIGHT]

Generate a 15-slide onboarding presentation:

1. **Welcome Slide** — Warm, personal welcome:
   - "Welcome to [COMPANY]" with a genuine, human message
   - Today's agenda
   - "By the end of today, you'll feel like [ASPIRATION]"

2. **Our Story** — Company origin and journey:
   - How and why the company was founded
   - Key milestones
   - Where we are today
   - Told as a story, not a timeline

3. **Our Mission** — Why we exist:
   - Mission statement
   - Real-world example of the mission in action
   - How every role contributes to the mission

4. **Our Values** — Values with teeth:
   - For each value:
     - What it means in practice (not just words)
     - A specific example of this value in action
     - How it shows up in daily work

5. **What We Do** — Products/services overview:
   - Customer-first explanation (what problems we solve)
   - Key products and who uses them
   - Market position and competitive context

6. **Who We Serve** — Customer overview:
   - Customer profiles
   - How customers use our product
   - Customer success stories
   - Why customers love us (and where we're improving)

7. **How We Work** — Culture and norms:
   - Communication norms (when to Slack vs. email vs. meet)
   - Meeting culture
   - Decision-making process
   - Feedback culture
   - Work-life balance expectations

8. **Your Team** — Team structure:
   - Org chart (or simplified view)
   - Your manager and skip-level
   - Key collaborators across teams
   - "Go-to" people for different needs

9. **Tools & Systems** — What you'll use daily:
   - Tool | Purpose | Setup instructions | Who to ask for help
   - Priority order: what to set up first

10. **Key Processes** — How things work here:
    - PTO and time off
    - Expense reporting
    - IT support
    - Performance reviews
    - Getting help or escalating issues

11. **Benefits Overview** — What's available to you:
    - Health/wellness
    - Learning and development
    - Perks and programs
    - Enrollment deadlines and how to sign up

12. **Your First Week** — Day by day:
    - Day 1: [WHAT HAPPENS]
    - Day 2: [WHAT HAPPENS]
    - Day 3-5: [WHAT HAPPENS]
    - Key meetings already scheduled
    - Your onboarding buddy: [NAME]

13. **Your First 30 Days** — Success metrics:
    - What "great" looks like at 30 days
    - Key milestones to hit
    - Conversations to have
    - Skills to develop

14. **FAQ** — Common new hire questions:
    - 8-10 actual FAQs with clear answers
    - "No question is too small" message

15. **Welcome Aboard** — Closing:
    - Encouraging closing message
    - Key contacts and resources
    - "You belong here" sentiment
    - Next step: what to do right after this session

Speaker notes should be warm, enthusiastic, and honest. New hires are nervous — make them feel like they made the right choice joining this company.
```

### Prompt 2 — Role-Specific Onboarding Training

Use this to create onboarding training specific to a particular role or department.

```
You are a training designer who creates role-specific onboarding programs. Create a role-specific onboarding presentation.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Role: [JOB TITLE]
Department: [DEPARTMENT]
Reports to: [MANAGER TITLE]
New hire's background (typical): [EXPECTED EXPERIENCE LEVEL AND BACKGROUND]

Role overview:
- Primary responsibilities: [LIST 4-6 KEY RESPONSIBILITIES]
- Key metrics they'll be measured on: [HOW SUCCESS IS DEFINED]
- Tools they'll use daily: [LIST ROLE-SPECIFIC TOOLS]
- Key stakeholders they'll work with: [INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL]
- Typical day/week: [DESCRIBE WHAT A TYPICAL WEEK LOOKS LIKE]

Skills needed:
- Technical skills: [LIST]
- Soft skills: [LIST]
- Company-specific knowledge: [WHAT THEY NEED TO LEARN ABOUT THE BUSINESS]

Onboarding milestones:
- Week 1: [WHAT THEY SHOULD KNOW/DO]
- Week 2-4: [WHAT THEY SHOULD KNOW/DO]
- Month 2: [WHAT THEY SHOULD KNOW/DO]
- Month 3: [WHAT THEY SHOULD KNOW/DO — SHOULD BE FULLY RAMPED]

Generate a 12-slide role onboarding deck:

1. **Role Overview** — "Here's what you'll do and why it matters":
   - Role in context: how this role fits into the team and company
   - Impact: what great performance in this role unlocks

2. **Your Responsibilities** — Clear breakdown:
   - Primary responsibilities (ranked by time spent)
   - Secondary responsibilities
   - What's NOT your responsibility (clarity prevents confusion)

3. **How You'll Be Measured** — Success metrics:
   - KPIs and metrics
   - How performance reviews work
   - What "exceeds expectations" looks like

4. **Your Tech Stack** — Tools of the trade:
   - Tool | What you use it for | Proficiency expected | Training available
   - Logins and access (how to get them)

5. **Your Team & Stakeholders** — Who you'll work with:
   - Direct team members and their roles
   - Cross-functional partners
   - External stakeholders (customers, vendors)
   - Relationship map

6. **Key Processes & Workflows** — How work flows:
   - For each major workflow:
     - Step-by-step process
     - Your role at each step
     - Handoff points
     - Where to find documentation

7. **A Day in the Life** — Typical day walkthrough:
   - Morning routine
   - Meetings and collaboration
   - Focus work
   - End-of-day rituals

8. **Common Scenarios** — Real situations you'll face:
   - Scenario 1: [SITUATION] → How to handle it
   - Scenario 2: [SITUATION] → How to handle it
   - Scenario 3: [SITUATION] → How to handle it
   - "When in doubt, ask [PERSON/CHANNEL]"

9. **Your Ramp Plan** — Week by week:
   - Week 1: Shadow, observe, ask questions
   - Week 2-4: Start doing with guidance
   - Month 2: Independent with check-ins
   - Month 3: Fully autonomous
   - For each phase: specific deliverables and learning goals

10. **Resources & Documentation** — Where to find things:
    - Knowledge base / wiki links
    - Process documentation
    - Training materials
    - "If you can't find it, ask [CHANNEL/PERSON]"

11. **Your Support Network**
    - Your manager: what they're responsible for in your onboarding
    - Your onboarding buddy: who and how to use them
    - Your team: how they'll help
    - HR/People Ops: what they handle

12. **Your First Assignment**
    - A specific, achievable first task or project
    - Why it matters (builds confidence and connection)
    - Resources available to complete it
    - Due date and who to submit to

Speaker notes should balance information with reassurance. Role onboarding should make someone feel equipped, not overwhelmed.
```

### Prompt 3 — Manager Onboarding / New Manager Training

Use this for training new managers or onboarding someone who has been promoted to a management role for the first time.

```
You are a leadership development expert. Create a new manager training presentation.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Audience: [NEWLY PROMOTED MANAGERS / FIRST-TIME MANAGERS / MANAGERS JOINING FROM OUTSIDE]
Number of participants: [GROUP SIZE]
Session length: [2 HOURS / HALF DAY / FULL DAY]
Company's management philosophy: [DESCRIBE IN 2-3 SENTENCES — e.g., "servant leadership," "high autonomy with accountability," "coaching-oriented"]

Management expectations at this company:
- [EXPECTATION 1 — e.g., "Weekly 1-on-1s with every direct report"]
- [EXPECTATION 2 — e.g., "Quarterly career development conversations"]
- [EXPECTATION 3 — e.g., "Responsible for team engagement scores"]
- [EXPECTATION 4 — e.g., "Manage performance issues within 30 days"]

Tools and systems managers use:
- [HR SYSTEM]
- [PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT TOOL]
- [COMMUNICATION TOOLS]
- [BUDGET/APPROVAL SYSTEMS]

Generate a 15-slide new manager training:

1. **Welcome to Management** — Frame the role positively:
   - "Being a manager is one of the most impactful things you'll ever do"
   - What changes (and what doesn't) when you become a manager
   - The manager's job in one sentence at this company

2. **Management Philosophy Here** — How we manage:
   - Company's approach to leadership
   - What "good management" looks like here
   - Examples of great managers in this organization and what they do

3. **Your New Responsibilities**
   - Things you're now responsible for that you weren't before
   - Things you should stop doing (individual contributor work to delegate)
   - Where to spend your time: the 70/20/10 rule for managers

4. **Building Trust with Your Team**
   - The trust equation: credibility + reliability + intimacy / self-orientation
   - First 30 days as a new manager: how to build relationships
   - Common trust-destroying mistakes to avoid

5. **Running Effective 1-on-1s**
   - Purpose of 1-on-1s (it's THEIR meeting, not yours)
   - Frequency and duration expectations
   - Template: questions to ask
   - Common 1-on-1 mistakes
   - How to document and follow up

6. **Giving Feedback**
   - The SBI framework: Situation → Behavior → Impact
   - Positive feedback: when and how
   - Constructive feedback: when and how
   - Practice scenarios (include 3 role-play scenarios)
   - The feedback conversation structure

7. **Having Difficult Conversations**
   - Performance concerns
   - Interpersonal conflicts
   - Compensation discussions
   - Career disappointment (not getting a promotion, etc.)
   - The conversation framework: Prepare → Open → Discuss → Commit → Follow up

8. **Performance Management**
   - Setting expectations clearly
   - Ongoing feedback vs. formal reviews
   - Performance review process at this company
   - Performance improvement plans: when and how
   - When to involve HR

9. **Coaching & Development**
   - The difference between managing and coaching
   - GROW model: Goal → Reality → Options → Will
   - Career development conversations: how to run them
   - Creating development plans

10. **Team Dynamics**
    - Building a healthy team culture
    - Managing diverse personalities and working styles
    - Team meetings: how to run them well
    - Conflict resolution within the team

11. **Hiring & Building Your Team**
    - The hiring process at this company
    - What to look for beyond skills
    - Interviewing best practices
    - Making the case for new headcount

12. **Managing Up & Across**
    - Working effectively with your manager
    - What your manager needs from you
    - Cross-functional collaboration
    - Representing your team in leadership discussions

13. **Common New Manager Mistakes**
    - Mistake 1: Trying to be everyone's friend (with correction)
    - Mistake 2: Doing the work instead of managing the work (with correction)
    - Mistake 3: Avoiding difficult conversations (with correction)
    - Mistake 4: Changing everything immediately (with correction)
    - Mistake 5: Not asking for help (with correction)

14. **Your Manager Toolkit**
    - Systems and tools to set up
    - Templates for 1-on-1s, feedback, performance reviews
    - Resources: books, courses, internal training
    - Your support network: HR business partner, manager peer group, mentors

15. **Your First 90 Days as Manager**
    - Days 1-30: Listen, learn, build relationships
    - Days 31-60: Establish rhythms (1-on-1s, team meetings, processes)
    - Days 61-90: Start driving improvements and set team goals
    - Check-in point with your manager at 90 days

Speaker notes should normalize the anxiety of becoming a manager. Everyone struggles with this transition. The best managers are the ones who keep learning.
```

## Skills Training Presentations

### Prompt 4 — Technical Skills Training Presentation

Use this to create a training presentation that teaches a specific technical skill or tool.

```
You are a technical trainer who makes complex topics accessible. Create a technical training presentation.

Topic: [TECHNICAL SKILL/TOOL — e.g., "Advanced Excel for Finance," "Python for Data Analysis," "Salesforce Administration," "AWS Fundamentals"]
Audience: [WHO — ROLE AND CURRENT SKILL LEVEL]
Audience skill level: [BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED]
Session length: [1 HOUR / 2 HOURS / HALF DAY / FULL DAY]
Format: [LECTURE + DEMO / HANDS-ON LAB / WORKSHOP / SELF-PACED]
Prerequisites: [WHAT ATTENDEES SHOULD ALREADY KNOW]

Learning objectives — by the end, participants should be able to:
- [OBJECTIVE 1 — use action verbs: create, configure, analyze, build]
- [OBJECTIVE 2]
- [OBJECTIVE 3]
- [OBJECTIVE 4]

Key topics to cover:
- [TOPIC 1]
- [TOPIC 2]
- [TOPIC 3]
- [TOPIC 4]
- [TOPIC 5]

Generate a training presentation:

1. **Opening (10 minutes)**
   - Why this skill matters for their role
   - What they'll be able to do by the end (concrete outcomes)
   - Quick poll: current experience level with this topic
   - Agenda and logistics

2. **Foundations (15-20 minutes)**
   - Core concepts explained simply
   - Mental model or framework for understanding the topic
   - Analogy that makes the concept click
   - Common misconceptions addressed

3. **Demonstration Sections** — For each key topic:
   - **Concept** (3-5 minutes): Explain what it is and why it matters
   - **Demo** (5-10 minutes): Step-by-step walkthrough
     - Exact steps to show
     - What to highlight on screen
     - Common pitfalls to point out
   - **Practice** (10-15 minutes): Hands-on exercise
     - Exercise instructions (clear, numbered steps)
     - Expected outcome
     - Bonus challenge for advanced participants
   - **Debrief** (3-5 minutes): Key takeaways and troubleshooting

4. **Real-World Application (15 minutes)**
   - How this skill applies to their actual work
   - 2-3 scenarios from their daily workflow
   - Tips for applying immediately after training

5. **Common Errors & Troubleshooting**
   - Top 5 mistakes beginners make
   - How to identify and fix each one
   - Error messages and what they mean
   - Where to find help

6. **Resources & Next Steps**
   - Reference guide / cheat sheet content
   - Practice exercises to do on their own
   - Advanced resources for going deeper
   - Internal experts to consult
   - Next training in the series (if applicable)

7. **Assessment** (optional)
   - 5-10 quiz questions to check understanding
   - Answers with explanations

Speaker notes should include timing for each section and tips for managing different skill levels in the same room.
```

### Prompt 5 — Soft Skills Training Presentation

Use this for training on communication, leadership, collaboration, time management, or other interpersonal skills.

```
You are a professional development trainer. Create a soft skills training presentation.

Skill: [SOFT SKILL — e.g., "Effective Communication," "Conflict Resolution," "Time Management," "Emotional Intelligence," "Presentation Skills," "Active Listening"]
Audience: [WHO — ROLE AND LEVEL]
Session length: [1 HOUR / 2 HOURS / HALF DAY]
Delivery: [IN-PERSON / VIRTUAL / HYBRID]
Group size: [NUMBER]
Current pain point: [WHY THIS TRAINING IS NEEDED — e.g., "team communication has broken down," "managers avoid difficult conversations," "meetings run over and nothing gets decided"]

Generate a training presentation:

1. **Opening — Why This Matters (10 minutes)**
   - A relatable story or scenario that illustrates the cost of poor [SKILL]
   - Research or data on the impact of this skill on career and business outcomes
   - Self-assessment: "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you in your [SKILL]?"
   - Learning objectives for the session

2. **The Framework (15 minutes)**
   - A simple, memorable model for the skill:
     - The model name (create something catchy and memorable)
     - 3-5 components of the model
     - Visual: how to draw/describe the model on one slide
   - Why this model works (research or evidence)
   - Common myths debunked

3. **Component Deep Dives (30-45 minutes)**
   For each component of the framework:
   - What it is and why it matters
   - What good looks like (positive example)
   - What bad looks like (negative example)
   - One specific technique to practice
   - Practice exercise:
     - Instructions for pairs or small groups
     - Duration (5-8 minutes)
     - Debrief questions

4. **Real Scenario Practice (20-30 minutes)**
   - 3 realistic scenarios from their workplace:
     - Scenario description
     - Role-play instructions
     - What to observe and practice
     - Debrief: what worked, what didn't, what to try differently
   - Group discussion on common challenges

5. **Common Pitfalls (10 minutes)**
   - 5 mistakes people make with this skill
   - For each: the mistake, why people make it, and the fix
   - Self-awareness check: "Which of these do you recognize in yourself?"

6. **Personal Action Plan (10 minutes)**
   - Template for a personal development plan:
     - "One thing I'll start doing: ___"
     - "One thing I'll stop doing: ___"
     - "One thing I'll continue doing: ___"
     - "I'll practice this week by: ___"
     - Accountability: share with a partner

7. **Resources & Continued Learning**
   - Book recommendations (1-2 key books)
   - Internal resources
   - Practice opportunities
   - Follow-up session date (if applicable)

Speaker notes should include facilitation tips: how to create a safe space for vulnerability, how to manage dominant and quiet participants, and how to handle resistance to soft skills training.
```

### Prompt 6 — Sales Training Presentation

Use this to create a sales training session for new or existing sales team members.

```
You are a sales training director. Create a sales training presentation.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Product: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Sales model: [INSIDE SALES / FIELD SALES / PRODUCT-LED / HYBRID]
Target buyer: [IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE]
Average deal size: [$AMOUNT]
Sales cycle: [TYPICAL LENGTH]
Audience: [NEW REPS / EXPERIENCED REPS / MANAGERS / MIXED]
Session length: [2 HOURS / HALF DAY / FULL DAY]
Focus area: [e.g., "discovery calls," "demo skills," "objection handling," "closing," "prospecting," "full sales methodology"]

Current sales methodology: [IF ANY — e.g., "MEDDIC," "Sandler," "Challenger," "none formally"]
Win rate: [CURRENT WIN RATE]
Top reasons we win: [LIST 3]
Top reasons we lose: [LIST 3]

Generate a sales training presentation:

1. **Our Sales DNA (15 minutes)**
   - What makes our sales approach unique
   - The buyer's journey for our product
   - What top performers do differently here
   - Success metrics and what "great" looks like

2. **Know Your Buyer (20 minutes)**
   - Ideal customer profile deep dive:
     - Company characteristics
     - Buyer persona (their daily life, goals, fears)
     - Buying triggers (what makes them ready to talk)
     - Decision process (who's involved, how long, what they need)
   - Exercise: profile your last 3 deals against this ICP

   Stage by stage:
   - Stage 1: [PROSPECTING/OUTREACH]

     - Templates and scripts
     - Activity targets

     - Active listening techniques

     - Customization techniques

     - Pricing presentation
     - Negotiation framework
   - Stage 5: [CLOSE]
     - Closing techniques
     - Timeline management
     - When to push and when to pause

4. **Objection Handling (30 minutes)**
   - Top 10 objections with:
     - The objection
     - What they really mean
     - Response framework
     - Example response (word for word)
     - Follow-up question
   - Practice: role-play exercise with partner

5. **Competitive Selling (15 minutes)**
   - Top 3 competitors: how to position against each
   - Battle cards for each competitor
   - Trap questions to ask prospects
   - What to say when the prospect mentions a competitor

6. **Tools & Technology (15 minutes)**
   - CRM best practices (what to log, how to forecast)
   - Sales engagement tools
   - Content and collateral available
   - When to use what

7. **Practice Scenarios (45 minutes)**
   - Scenario 1: Cold outreach to ideal prospect (practice + debrief)
   - Scenario 2: Discovery call (practice + debrief)
   - Scenario 3: Handling the toughest objection (practice + debrief)
   - Peer feedback framework

8. **Action Plan**
   - Top 3 skills to practice this week
   - Specific deals to apply new techniques to
   - Follow-up session for coaching

Speaker notes should include coaching cues for the trainer: what to listen for in role plays, how to give constructive feedback, and how to celebrate improvement.
```

## Workshop Facilitation

### Prompt 7 — Strategy Workshop Presentation

Use this to design and facilitate a strategy workshop for a team or leadership group.

```
You are a strategy workshop facilitator. Design a complete strategy workshop.

Workshop purpose: [e.g., "annual strategic planning," "new market entry decision," "product strategy alignment," "team goal setting"]
Participants: [WHO — ROLES AND NUMBER]
Duration: [HALF DAY / FULL DAY / 2 DAYS]
Location: [IN-PERSON / VIRTUAL / HYBRID]
Materials available: [WHITEBOARDS / STICKY NOTES / LAPTOPS / MIRO BOARD / PRINTED WORKSHEETS]

Context:
- Current business situation: [BRIEF CONTEXT]
- Key decisions to make: [WHAT NEEDS TO BE DECIDED OR ALIGNED ON]
- Existing data/analysis: [WHAT PREP MATERIAL EXISTS — e.g., market research, competitive analysis, customer feedback]
- Constraints: [BUDGET, TIME, RESOURCES]

Generate a complete workshop plan:

1. **Pre-Work** — What participants should do before the workshop:
   - Pre-read materials (what to review)
   - Pre-workshop survey or questions to reflect on
   - Data to bring or review

2. **Opening (30 minutes)**
   - Welcome and framing: why we're here, what we'll accomplish
   - Ground rules for productive discussion
   - Icebreaker connected to the workshop theme (not generic)
   - Agenda overview with participant expectations

3. **Context Setting (45 minutes)**
   - Present key data and insights (slide content for this section)
   - Market/competitive landscape overview
   - Customer insights summary
   - Current performance data
   - "State of the world" that informs our strategy

4. **Divergent Thinking Sessions (90 minutes)**
   - Exercise 1: Brainstorming / ideation
     - Format: individual → pairs → groups
     - Prompt questions (specific, not generic)
     - How to capture ideas (sticky notes, digital board)
     - Time allocation for each phase
   - Exercise 2: Scenario planning or "what if" analysis
     - 3 scenarios to explore
     - Group assignments
     - Discussion framework

5. **Convergent Thinking Sessions (90 minutes)**
   - Exercise 3: Prioritization
     - Framework: impact vs. effort matrix, MoSCoW, or dot voting
     - How to run the voting/ranking process
     - Criteria for prioritization
   - Exercise 4: Decision making
     - Framework for making decisions as a group
     - How to handle disagreements
     - Commitment process

6. **Action Planning (60 minutes)**
   - Exercise 5: From strategy to action
     - Template: Initiative → Owner → Timeline → Success Metric → Resources Needed
     - How to break big strategies into 90-day sprints
     - Accountability framework

7. **Closing (30 minutes)**
   - Summary of decisions made
   - Review of action items and owners
   - Commitment ceremony (everyone states their #1 priority)
   - Next meeting date for check-in
   - Feedback on the workshop

8. **Facilitator Guide**
   - Detailed facilitation notes for each section
   - How to manage energy levels throughout the day
   - How to handle dominant voices
   - How to handle conflict
   - Timing flex: where to cut or extend if needed
   - Backup exercises if something doesn't work

9. **Workshop Materials**
   - Content for all worksheets/templates
   - Slide content for facilitator presentations
   - Printed agenda for participants
   - Sticky note prompts

A great strategy workshop produces decisions and action plans, not just discussion. Design every minute to move toward those outcomes.
```

### Prompt 8 — Design Thinking Workshop

Use this to run a design thinking or innovation workshop for any team.

```
You are a design thinking facilitator. Create a design thinking workshop presentation and guide.

Workshop focus: [WHAT PROBLEM OR OPPORTUNITY TO APPLY DESIGN THINKING TO — e.g., "improve customer onboarding experience," "design a new feature," "redesign internal workflow"]
Participants: [WHO AND HOW MANY]
Duration: [3 HOURS / HALF DAY / FULL DAY]
User/customer context: [WHO ARE WE DESIGNING FOR]
Materials: [WHAT'S AVAILABLE — sticky notes, markers, whiteboards, craft supplies, laptops]

Generate a workshop plan covering all 5 design thinking phases:

1. **Introduction (20 minutes)**
   - What is design thinking and why we're using it
   - The 5 phases overview (empathize → define → ideate → prototype → test)
   - Ground rules: "Yes, and..." mentality, defer judgment, build on others' ideas
   - Warm-up exercise: creative confidence builder

2. **Phase 1: Empathize (45-60 minutes)**
   - Exercise: Empathy mapping
     - Template with 4 quadrants: Says, Thinks, Does, Feels
     - How to fill it in using existing customer data or role-play
     - 15 minutes individual, 15 minutes group synthesis
   - Exercise: User journey mapping
     - Steps in the current experience
     - Emotional highs and lows
     - Pain points and moments of delight
   - Debrief: What surprised us about the user's experience?

3. **Phase 2: Define (30-40 minutes)**
   - Exercise: Problem statement creation
     - "How might we..." framework
     - Take the empathy insights and reframe as opportunities
     - Each group creates 3-5 HMW statements
     - Voting/prioritization: pick the most promising 1-2 problems to solve
   - Output: Clear problem statement the team aligns on

4. **Phase 3: Ideate (45-60 minutes)**
   - Exercise: Crazy 8s (rapid sketching)
     - Fold paper into 8 sections
     - 1 minute per section: sketch a solution idea
     - Share and explain top 2-3 ideas
   - Exercise: Idea building
     - Take the best ideas from Crazy 8s
     - Groups expand on one idea together
     - How to combine elements from different ideas
   - Dot voting to select ideas to prototype

5. **Phase 4: Prototype (45-60 minutes)**
   - What prototyping means (not a finished product — just enough to test)
   - Prototype options:
     - Paper prototypes / sketches
     - Role-playing / acting it out
     - Storyboards
     - Simple digital mockups
   - Groups build prototypes of their selected idea
   - Focus: what's the core concept and how do you communicate it?

6. **Phase 5: Test (30-45 minutes)**
   - Groups present prototypes to each other
   - Feedback framework:
     - "I like..." (what works)
     - "I wish..." (what could be better)
```

```
   - Common shortcuts that create risk

4. **Emergency Procedures**
   - Emergency exits and routes (specific to building/location)
   - Assembly points
   - Who to call and in what order
   - First aid locations and trained personnel
   - What to do if you discover an emergency

5. **Reporting**
   - How to report a safety concern
   - How to report an incident (even minor ones)
   - Anonymous reporting options
   - Non-retaliation policy
   - "See something, say something" culture

6. **Scenario Quiz**
   - 5 scenario-based questions:
     - "You notice [SITUATION]. What do you do?"
     - Group discussion or individual response
   - Correct answers with explanation

7. **Quick Reference**
   - Emergency numbers
   - Safety contact
   - First aid kit locations
   - Fire extinguisher locations
   - PPE requirements summary

8. **Acknowledgment**
   - Training completion acknowledgment
   - Commitment: "I understand my role in workplace safety"
   - Next refresher date

Speaker notes should be serious without being scary. Safety training works when people understand the real risks and feel empowered to prevent incidents.
```

```
   - What they expect from support

   - How to find answers you don't know
   - When to escalate (and to whom)
```

### Prompt 20 — Change Adoption Training

Use this when rolling out a new tool, process, or way of working and you need to train people to adopt it.

```
You are a change adoption specialist. Create a training presentation for rolling out a new tool/process/system to a team.

What's changing: [DESCRIBE THE CHANGE — e.g., "migrating from Slack to Teams," "new CRM implementation," "new expense reporting process," "new project management methodology"]
Why it's changing: [BUSINESS REASON]
Who's affected: [AUDIENCE]
Timeline: [WHEN THIS GOES LIVE]
Training session length: [30 MIN / 60 MIN / 90 MIN]
Current tool/process being replaced: [WHAT THEY USE NOW]
Level of resistance expected: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH — AND WHY]

New tool/process details:
- Key features: [WHAT IT DOES]
- How it's different from the current approach: [KEY DIFFERENCES]
- Benefits for the individual user: [WHAT'S IN IT FOR THEM]
- What they'll need to learn: [KEY SKILLS/KNOWLEDGE]

Generate a training presentation:

1. **Why We're Changing (10 minutes)**
   - The business need (honest, not corporate-speak)
   - What's not working about the current approach
   - What the new approach enables
   - WIIFM (What's In It For Me?) — personal benefits for the audience
   - Address the elephant in the room: "Change is uncomfortable. That's normal."

2. **What's Changing (and What's Not) (5 minutes)**
   - Specific changes listed clearly
   - What stays the same (stability anchors)
   - Timeline: when each change takes effect

3. **The New [TOOL/PROCESS] (15-20 minutes)**
   - Overview: what it is and how it works
   - Side-by-side comparison: "Old way → New way" for common tasks
   - Live demo or screenshot walkthrough:
     - Task 1: How to [COMMON TASK] — step by step
     - Task 2: How to [COMMON TASK]
     - Task 3: How to [COMMON TASK]
   - Tips: what's easier in the new system

4. **Hands-On Practice (15-20 minutes)**
   - Guided exercises:
     - Exercise 1: [BASIC TASK TO TRY]
     - Exercise 2: [INTERMEDIATE TASK]
     - Exercise 3: [MOST COMMON WORKFLOW END-TO-END]
   - Support available during practice (who to call over for help)

5. **Common Questions & Concerns (10 minutes)**
   - FAQ: the questions everyone is thinking but might not ask:
     - "What happens to my old data?"
     - "What if I can't figure it out?"
     - "Why didn't we pick [ALTERNATIVE]?"
     - "What if this doesn't work?"
   - Honest answers for each

6. **Support & Resources (5 minutes)**
   - Training recordings/materials
   - Quick reference guide
   - "Go-to" person for questions
   - Feedback channel: "Tell us what's working and what's not"
   - Additional training sessions available

7. **Your Next Steps (5 minutes)**
   - What to do this week to prepare
   - When the old system turns off (hard deadline)
   - First thing to try in the new system
   - Who to contact if you get stuck

8. **Closing**
   - Acknowledgment: "Learning something new takes patience. We're in this together."
   - Commitment from leadership to support the transition
   - "You will get this. And we'll help."

Speaker notes should be empathetic to resistance. People resist change not because they're stubborn, but because they're competent in the current system and feel incompetent in the new one. Honor that feeling while building confidence.
```

### Prompt 21 — Leadership Development Program Presentation

Use this to create a presentation for a leadership development program or emerging leaders course.

```
You are a leadership development expert. Create a session for a leadership development program.

Program name: [PROGRAM NAME — OR CREATE ONE]
Session topic: [SPECIFIC LEADERSHIP TOPIC — e.g., "Leading Through Uncertainty," "Building High-Performance Teams," "Strategic Thinking," "Influence Without Authority"]
Audience: [EMERGING LEADERS / MID-LEVEL MANAGERS / SENIOR LEADERS]
Group size: [NUMBER]
Session length: [2 HOURS / HALF DAY]
Company context: [CURRENT COMPANY CHALLENGES OR PRIORITIES RELEVANT TO THIS TOPIC]

Generate a leadership development session:

1. **Opening Reflection (10 minutes)**
   - A leadership challenge or dilemma to consider:
     - Present a scenario that has no easy answer
     - Individual reflection: "What would you do?"
     - Pair share: compare approaches
   - This sets the theme for the session

2. **Framework Introduction (20 minutes)**
   - A leadership framework for this topic:
     - The model (with a visual description)
     - Research behind it
     - Why it matters specifically for leaders at their level
     - How it connects to the company's current challenges

3. **Self-Assessment (15 minutes)**
   - Assessment exercise:
     - 10-15 questions related to the framework
     - Self-score
     - Identify top strength and top development area
     - Optional: compare self-assessment with peer feedback

4. **Case Study (30 minutes)**
   - A detailed leadership case study relevant to the topic:
     - The situation (company, leader, challenge)
     - The context and constraints
     - What the leader did
     - The outcome
   - Small group discussion questions:
     - "What did the leader do well?"
     - "What would you have done differently?"
     - "How does this apply to your current situation?"
   - Group debrief

5. **Skill Practice (30 minutes)**
   - 2 role-play or practice exercises:
     - Exercise 1: Practice the skill in a simulated conversation
     - Exercise 2: Apply the framework to a real challenge from participants' lives
   - Peer coaching: partners give feedback using a provided rubric

6. **Personal Application (15 minutes)**
   - "How does this apply to YOUR leadership right now?"
   - Individual action planning:
     - One behavior to start
     - One behavior to stop
     - One conversation to have this week
   - Accountability partner assignment

7. **Closing (10 minutes)**
   - Key takeaways
   - Connection to next session in the program
   - Recommended reading or resources
   - "Leadership is a practice, not a destination"

Speaker notes should create a safe space for leaders to be vulnerable about their challenges. The best leadership development happens when people can be honest about where they struggle.
```

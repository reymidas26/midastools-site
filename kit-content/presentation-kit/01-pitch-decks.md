# 01 — Startup Pitch Deck Prompts

## 25+ AI Prompts for Investor Pitch Decks That Get Funded

These prompts help you create compelling pitch deck content — from your opening slide to your closing ask. Each prompt generates slide-ready text, speaker notes, and supporting content for investor presentations.

## Problem Slide

### Prompt 1 — Problem Statement That Creates Urgency

Use this to create a problem slide that makes investors immediately understand the pain point and feel the urgency of solving it.

```
You are an expert pitch deck consultant who has helped startups raise over $500M in funding. Create compelling problem slide content for a startup pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY — e.g., fintech, healthtech, edtech, SaaS]
Target customer: [TARGET CUSTOMER — e.g., small business owners, enterprise HR teams, freelance designers]
Core problem: [DESCRIBE THE MAIN PROBLEM YOUR PRODUCT SOLVES IN 1-2 SENTENCES]

Generate the following:

1. **Headline** — A punchy, emotional one-liner for the slide title (under 10 words)
2. **3 Pain Points** — Three specific, quantifiable pain points the target customer faces. Use real-world statistics or credible estimates where possible.
3. **Status Quo Statement** — One sentence describing how people currently deal with this problem (and why it's inadequate)
4. **Emotional Hook** — A brief story or scenario (2-3 sentences) that makes the audience feel the problem viscerally
5. **Speaker Notes** — 60-second talking track for presenting this slide, written in conversational tone

Format each section with clear headers. The tone should be urgent but not alarmist — investors should think "yes, this is a real problem worth solving."
```

### Prompt 2 — Problem Validation With Data

Use this when you need to back up your problem slide with market data, research, and credible third-party validation.

```
You are a startup pitch consultant specializing in data-driven storytelling. I need to validate the problem my startup solves with compelling data for an investor pitch deck.

Startup: [COMPANY NAME]
Problem we solve: [DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM IN 2-3 SENTENCES]
Target market: [TARGET MARKET — e.g., US small businesses, enterprise SaaS companies, healthcare providers]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]

Generate the following:

1. **3-5 Statistics** that quantify the severity of this problem. For each stat:
   - The data point (with a plausible source or industry report)
   - A one-sentence interpretation of why this stat matters to investors
2. **Market Trend** — Describe one macro trend making this problem worse (not better), showing urgency
3. **Customer Quote Template** — Write a fictional but realistic quote from a target customer describing this pain, formatted as a testimonial I can replace with a real one later: "[Quote]" — [First Name], [Title] at [Company Type]
4. **Comparison Data** — Show the cost of the problem (time, money, or opportunity cost) vs. the cost of not solving it
5. **Slide Layout Recommendation** — Suggest how to visually arrange this data on a single slide for maximum impact

Keep all numbers realistic for the [INDUSTRY] space. If exact figures aren't available, use credible ranges with qualifiers like "estimated" or "industry reports suggest."
```

## Solution Slide

### Prompt 3 — Solution Slide That Clicks Instantly

Use this to explain your solution in a way that creates an immediate "aha" moment for investors.

```
You are a pitch deck strategist who helps founders explain complex products simply. Create solution slide content for an investor pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Problem: [ONE-SENTENCE PROBLEM STATEMENT]
Solution: [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE IN 2-3 SENTENCES]
How it works (briefly): [HIGH-LEVEL DESCRIPTION OF HOW THE PRODUCT WORKS]
Key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES YOUR SOLUTION UNIQUE]

Generate:

1. **Headline** — A clear, benefit-driven slide title (under 10 words) that makes the solution click
2. **One-Liner** — A single sentence explaining what you do, using the format: "We help [WHO] do [WHAT] by [HOW]"
3. **3 Key Benefits** — Three benefits of the solution (not features), each as a bold header + one supporting sentence. Focus on outcomes the customer gets.
4. **Before/After** — A quick before/after comparison showing life without your product vs. with it (2-3 bullet points each side)
5. **Analogy or Metaphor** — One analogy that makes the solution instantly understandable (e.g., "We're Stripe for [X]" or "Think of it as [familiar concept] meets [familiar concept]")
6. **Speaker Notes** — 45-second talking track that walks through the solution naturally, as if explaining to a smart friend

Avoid jargon. An investor who knows nothing about [INDUSTRY] should understand this immediately.
```

### Prompt 4 — Product Demo Walkthrough Script

Use this to create a scripted walkthrough of your product for the demo portion of your pitch.

```
You are a product demo expert who coaches founders on live demos during investor pitches. Create a product walkthrough script for my pitch deck.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it does: [2-3 SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Key features to show: [LIST 3-5 KEY FEATURES]
Demo format: [LIVE DEMO / SCREENSHOTS / VIDEO / ANIMATED MOCKUPS]
Time available: [1 MINUTE / 2 MINUTES / 3 MINUTES]

Generate:

1. **Demo Opening** — A 10-second setup line that tells investors exactly what they're about to see and why it matters
2. **Step-by-Step Script** — A narrated walkthrough covering each key feature:
   - What to show on screen
   - What to say (exact words)
   - The "wow moment" or key insight for each step
3. **Transition Phrases** — Smooth transitions between each feature/step
4. **Demo Closing** — A 10-second wrap-up that ties the demo back to the business opportunity
5. **Backup Plan** — What to say if the demo breaks or doesn't load (a confident pivot)
6. **Timing Notes** — Estimated seconds for each section totaling [TIME AVAILABLE]

The script should feel natural and conversational, not robotic. Build excitement progressively — save the most impressive feature for the middle or end.
```

## Market Opportunity

### Prompt 5 — TAM/SAM/SOM Market Sizing Slide

Use this to create a credible, bottom-up market sizing slide that investors will actually believe.

```
You are a market research analyst who specializes in helping startups build credible market sizing for investor presentations. Create TAM/SAM/SOM content for a pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Product/Service: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Target customer: [SPECIFIC TARGET CUSTOMER]
Price point: [YOUR PRICING — e.g., $49/month per user, $5,000/year per company]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Geography (current): [CURRENT MARKET — e.g., US only, North America, global]
Geography (future): [EXPANSION PLANS]

Generate:

1. **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — The total market if everyone who could theoretically buy your product did. Show the calculation methodology (top-down reference + bottom-up validation). Provide a dollar figure and brief explanation.

2. **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)** — The segment of TAM you can actually reach with your current product and go-to-market. Show the narrowing criteria and dollar figure.

3. **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** — What you can realistically capture in 3-5 years. Show the assumptions (market share %, penetration rate) and dollar figure.

4. **Market Growth Rate** — Annual growth rate of this market with supporting context (what's driving growth)

5. **Why Now** — 3 reasons this market is ready for disruption right now (technology shifts, regulatory changes, behavioral changes, etc.)

6. **Slide Copy** — The exact text and numbers to put on the slide, formatted as a TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown with a recommended visual layout (concentric circles, bar chart, etc.)

7. **Speaker Notes** — 45-second talking track emphasizing the bottom-up calculation and "why now" narrative

Be specific with numbers. Use credible-sounding methodology. Investors see through hand-wavy market sizing instantly.
```

### Prompt 6 — Market Landscape and Positioning Slide

Use this to show investors you understand the competitive landscape and have a clear position within it.

```
You are a competitive strategy consultant helping a startup create a market landscape slide for their investor pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Product: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION IN ONE SENTENCE]
Category: [MARKET CATEGORY — e.g., project management software, AI writing tools]
Main competitors: [LIST 3-5 COMPETITORS]
Our key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES US DIFFERENT]
Target segment we own: [THE SPECIFIC NICHE OR SEGMENT WE DOMINATE]

Generate:

1. **Landscape Headline** — A slide title that frames the market in a way that makes your positioning obvious

2. **2x2 Matrix** — Define two axes that create a 2x2 matrix where your company sits alone in the best quadrant. Specify:
   - X-axis label and what low/high means
   - Y-axis label and what low/high means
   - Where each competitor falls
   - Where you fall (top-right or equivalent "best" quadrant)

3. **Positioning Statement** — One sentence: "Unlike [COMPETITOR CATEGORY], [COMPANY] is the only [SOLUTION] that [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR] for [TARGET CUSTOMER]"

4. **3 Competitive Advantages** — Three specific advantages you have, framed as moats (not just features):
   - What the advantage is
   - Why it's hard to replicate
   - How it compounds over time

5. **Speaker Notes** — 45-second talking track that acknowledges competitors respectfully while making your differentiation crystal clear

Never trash competitors. Show that you have a deep understanding of the market and a unique, defensible position.
```

## Business Model

### Prompt 7 — Business Model and Revenue Slide

Use this to clearly explain how you make money and why the unit economics make sense.

```
You are a startup financial strategist helping create a business model slide for an investor pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Revenue model: [HOW YOU CHARGE — e.g., SaaS subscription, marketplace commission, usage-based, freemium]
Pricing: [YOUR PRICING TIERS OR STRUCTURE]
Target customer: [WHO PAYS YOU]
Current revenue (if any): [CURRENT MRR/ARR OR "PRE-REVENUE"]
Key costs: [YOUR MAIN COST DRIVERS]
Industry benchmarks: [ANY RELEVANT BENCHMARKS — e.g., typical SaaS margins, marketplace take rates]

Generate:

1. **Headline** — A slide title that conveys an attractive business model in under 10 words

2. **Revenue Model Overview** — 2-3 sentences clearly explaining how money flows from customer to you

3. **Unit Economics** — A clear breakdown:
   - Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) — actual or estimated
   - Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) — monthly and annual
   - Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) — with assumptions stated
   - LTV:CAC Ratio — with context on why this is attractive
   - Payback Period — months to recoup CAC
   - Gross Margin — percentage with brief explanation

4. **Revenue Streams** — If multiple revenue streams, list each with percentage of total revenue (actual or projected)

5. **Pricing Strategy Rationale** — 2-3 sentences on why your pricing is set where it is and room for expansion (upsells, price increases, new products)

6. **Financial Projections Preview** — A 3-year revenue projection table (Year 1, Year 2, Year 3) with key assumptions listed

7. **Speaker Notes** — 60-second talking track covering the business model, emphasizing unit economics and path to profitability

If pre-revenue, focus on projected unit economics based on market benchmarks and early signals.
```

### Prompt 8 — Go-to-Market Strategy Slide

Use this to show investors your plan for customer acquisition and growth.

```
You are a growth strategy consultant helping a startup create a go-to-market slide for their investor pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Product: [PRODUCT — ONE SENTENCE]
Target customer: [IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE]
Current stage: [PRE-LAUNCH / EARLY TRACTION / SCALING]
Current acquisition channels: [LIST CURRENT CHANNELS — e.g., organic search, cold outreach, partnerships]
Budget for growth: [MONTHLY MARKETING BUDGET OR "BOOTSTRAPPED"]
Sales model: [SELF-SERVE / INSIDE SALES / FIELD SALES / PRODUCT-LED GROWTH / HYBRID]

Generate:

1. **GTM Headline** — A confident slide title conveying strategic clarity

2. **Phase 1 (Now - 6 months)** — 3-4 specific tactics with expected outcomes:
   - Channel name
   - What you'll do specifically
   - Expected CAC range
   - Target customer volume

3. **Phase 2 (6-18 months)** — 3-4 expansion tactics as you grow:
   - New channels to add
   - Partnerships or integrations to pursue
   - How the strategy evolves with scale

4. **Phase 3 (18-36 months)** — Long-term GTM vision:
   - Market expansion (new segments, geographies)
   - Channel mix at maturity
   - Brand and community strategy

5. **Flywheel Effect** — Describe how your GTM creates a compounding advantage (e.g., user-generated content drives SEO, which drives signups, which drives more content)

6. **Key Metrics to Track** — 5 GTM metrics you'll report on and optimize

7. **Speaker Notes** — 45-second talking track emphasizing traction so far and the logical path to scale

Be specific about tactics, not generic. "Content marketing" is not a strategy — "publishing 3 SEO-optimized comparison articles per week targeting [KEYWORD CLUSTER]" is.
```

## Traction & Metrics

### Prompt 9 — Traction Slide That Proves Momentum

Use this to showcase your traction in a way that builds investor confidence and shows an upward trajectory.

```
You are an investor relations expert who helps startups present traction data compellingly. Create a traction slide for a pitch deck.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Stage: [PRE-SEED / SEED / SERIES A / SERIES B]
Key metrics:
- Revenue: [CURRENT MRR/ARR AND GROWTH RATE]
- Users/Customers: [NUMBER AND GROWTH RATE]
- Engagement: [KEY ENGAGEMENT METRIC — e.g., DAU/MAU ratio, session time, retention rate]
- Other notable metrics: [ANY OTHER IMPRESSIVE NUMBERS — NPS, conversion rate, etc.]

Timeline: [WHEN YOU LAUNCHED / KEY MILESTONES WITH DATES]
Notable customers or logos: [ANY RECOGNIZABLE NAMES — OR "NONE YET"]
Key achievements: [AWARDS, PRESS, PARTNERSHIPS, WAITLIST SIZE, ETC.]

Generate:

1. **Headline** — A slide title that leads with your strongest metric (e.g., "3x Revenue Growth in 6 Months")

2. **Metric Showcase** — 4-6 key metrics displayed as large numbers with labels and growth indicators:
   - Format: [METRIC NAME]: [NUMBER] ([GROWTH %] MoM/QoQ)
   - Order from most impressive to supporting metrics

3. **Growth Narrative** — 3-4 sentences telling the story of your traction: what happened, what drove it, and what it signals about product-market fit

4. **Timeline/Milestones** — A chronological list of 5-8 key milestones with dates, showing accelerating progress

5. **Social Proof** — How to present logos, testimonials, or press mentions on this slide

6. **What's Next** — One sentence teasing the next major milestone you're about to hit

7. **Speaker Notes** — 60-second talking track that uses traction data to build a "this is working and accelerating" narrative

Lead with the metric investors care most about for your stage. Pre-seed = engagement and retention. Seed = revenue growth rate. Series A = absolute revenue and unit economics.
```

### Prompt 16 — Competitive Advantage Deep Dive

Use this when you need to articulate your competitive moat clearly for sophisticated investors.

```
You are a strategy consultant who helps startups articulate defensible competitive advantages. Create content for a competitive advantage slide and talking points.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Product: [WHAT YOU DO]
Competitors: [LIST TOP 3-5 COMPETITORS AND WHAT THEY DO]
Our current advantages: [LIST WHAT YOU THINK YOUR ADVANTAGES ARE]
Our potential vulnerabilities: [WHAT COULD A COMPETITOR DO TO BEAT YOU?]

Generate:

1. **Moat Classification** — Identify which types of moats you have or are building:
   - Network effects (and how they compound)
   - Switching costs (and what creates them)
   - Data advantages (and why more data = better product)
   - Brand/trust (and how you're building it)
   - Technical/IP (and what's proprietary)
   - Cost advantages (and what drives them)
   - Regulatory/compliance (and what's hard to replicate)

2. **Competitive Comparison Table** — A feature/capability comparison matrix:
   - Rows: 5-7 key evaluation criteria (pick the ones where you win)
   - Columns: You + top 3 competitors
   - Use checkmarks, partial, and X marks

3. **"Why Not" Answers** — For each major competitor, a 2-sentence answer to "Why can't [COMPETITOR] just build this?"

4. **Moat Over Time** — 3-sentence description of how your competitive advantage grows stronger over 1, 3, and 5 years

5. **Speaker Notes** — 45-second talking track on competitive positioning

Never be arrogant about competitors. Show you respect them while clearly demonstrating why you win in your target segment.
```

## Specialized Pitch Formats

### Prompt 17 — 3-Minute Pitch Script (Demo Day / Competition)

Use this for startup competitions, demo days, or any format where you have exactly 3 minutes.

```
You are a pitch coach who has prepared founders for Y Combinator Demo Day, TechCrunch Disrupt, and top startup competitions. Write a 3-minute pitch script.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
What we do: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Problem: [THE PROBLEM IN 2 SENTENCES]
Solution: [HOW WE SOLVE IT IN 2 SENTENCES]
Traction: [KEY TRACTION METRICS — 2-3 DATA POINTS]
Market size: [TAM FIGURE]
Business model: [HOW YOU MAKE MONEY — ONE SENTENCE]
Team: [FOUNDING TEAM CREDENTIALS — ONE SENTENCE EACH]
Ask: [WHAT YOU WANT FROM THE AUDIENCE]

Generate a complete 3-minute pitch script with:

1. **Opening Hook (15 seconds)** — A surprising statement, bold claim, or relatable scenario that grabs attention instantly
2. **Problem (30 seconds)** — Paint the problem vividly with one specific example
3. **Solution (30 seconds)** — Explain the product simply with the key "magic moment"
4. **Demo/Proof (30 seconds)** — What to show or describe that proves it works
5. **Traction (20 seconds)** — Rapid-fire metrics that build credibility
6. **Market (15 seconds)** — Size of the opportunity in one powerful statement
7. **Business Model (15 seconds)** — How you make money, simply
8. **Team (15 seconds)** — Why you're the team to do this
9. **Ask + Close (10 seconds)** — The specific ask with a memorable closing line

Include timing markers throughout. Mark where to pause, where to speed up, and where to make eye contact with the audience. Total word count should be approximately 450 words (standard 3-minute speaking pace).
```

### Prompt 21 — Pitch Deck Feedback and Improvement

Use this to get AI feedback on your existing pitch deck content and identify weaknesses.

```
You are a VC partner who reviews 20 pitch decks per week. I'm going to share my pitch deck content and I want your brutally honest feedback.

[PASTE YOUR CURRENT PITCH DECK TEXT/CONTENT HERE — slide by slide]

For each slide, provide:

1. **Strength** — What's working well on this slide
2. **Weakness** — The biggest issue with this slide
3. **Rewrite** — A suggested rewrite of the key content
4. **Investor Reaction** — What a typical investor is thinking when they see this slide

Then provide an overall assessment:

5. **Top 3 Strengths** of the overall deck
6. **Top 3 Weaknesses** that would cause an investor to pass
7. **Missing Elements** — What slides or content are missing that should be added
8. **Narrative Flow** — Does the story build logically? Where does it break?
9. **The "Pass" Risk** — The single most likely reason an investor would pass after seeing this deck
10. **Priority Fixes** — Ordered list of the 5 most impactful improvements, starting with the highest-impact change

Be specific and actionable. "Make it better" is not useful feedback. "Replace the generic market stat on slide 3 with your specific customer data showing 40% MoM growth" is.
```

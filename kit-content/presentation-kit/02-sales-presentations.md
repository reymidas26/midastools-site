# 02 — Sales Presentation Prompts

## 25+ AI Prompts for Sales Decks That Close Deals

These prompts help you create persuasive sales presentations — from discovery calls to final proposals. Each prompt generates slide-ready content, talking points, and objection-handling frameworks that move prospects toward a buying decision.

**Works with:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

## Discovery & Needs Assessment Presentations

### Prompt 1 — Discovery Call Presentation

Use this to create a structured presentation for your first meeting with a prospective customer. The goal is to establish credibility, uncover needs, and set up the next meeting.

```
You are a top-performing enterprise sales consultant. Create a discovery call presentation for an initial meeting with a prospect.

My company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE — 1-2 SENTENCES]
Industry we serve: [TARGET INDUSTRY]
Prospect company: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Prospect's industry: [THEIR INDUSTRY]
Prospect's likely challenges: [WHAT YOU THINK THEIR PAIN POINTS ARE]
Meeting format: [IN-PERSON / VIDEO CALL / PHONE]
Meeting duration: [30 MIN / 45 MIN / 60 MIN]

Generate a discovery presentation with:

1. **Opening Slide — Agenda** (2 minutes)
   - A proposed agenda that shows respect for their time
   - A question to confirm the agenda works: "Does this cover what you were hoping to discuss?"

2. **Credibility Slide** (3 minutes)
   - Your company in 30 seconds (not a company history — a relevant capability overview)
   - 2-3 logos or proof points that match their industry
   - One sentence on why you requested this meeting

3. **Industry Insight Slide** (5 minutes)
   - 2-3 trends or challenges affecting their industry right now
   - A thought-provoking question or statistic that shows you understand their world
   - Transition: "We're seeing companies like yours face [CHALLENGE]. Is that resonating?"

4. **Discovery Questions Framework** (15-20 minutes)
   - 10 discovery questions organized by category:
     - Current state (how they do things today)
     - Pain points (what's not working)
     - Impact (what the problem costs them)
     - Decision process (how they evaluate solutions)
     - Timeline (urgency and deadlines)
   - For each question: the question, why you're asking it, and what to listen for

5. **Bridge Slide** (3 minutes)
   - How to transition from discovery to a brief product teaser
   - A 30-second positioning statement customized to what you've learned

6. **Next Steps Slide** (2 minutes)
   - How to propose a follow-up meeting
   - What you'll prepare for them
   - Specific CTA language

Include speaker notes throughout with coaching on tone, pace, and active listening cues.
```

### Prompt 2 — Needs Assessment Summary Presentation

Use this after a discovery call to present back what you learned and confirm you understand the prospect's needs before proposing a solution.

```
You are a consultative sales expert. Create a "needs assessment summary" presentation to share with a prospect after a discovery meeting.

My company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Their industry: [INDUSTRY]
What they told us during discovery:
- Current situation: [HOW THEY CURRENTLY HANDLE THE PROBLEM]
- Key pain points: [LIST 3-5 PAIN POINTS THEY MENTIONED]
- Desired outcomes: [WHAT THEY WANT TO ACHIEVE]
- Decision criteria: [WHAT MATTERS TO THEM IN A SOLUTION]
- Timeline: [THEIR TIMELINE FOR A DECISION]
- Budget context: [ANY BUDGET INFO SHARED]
- Stakeholders: [WHO ELSE IS INVOLVED IN THE DECISION]

Generate a 6-8 slide presentation:

1. **Title Slide** — "[PROSPECT NAME] + [YOUR COMPANY]: Understanding Your Needs"

2. **Meeting Recap Slide** — Brief summary of the conversation framed as "What We Heard"
   - 4-5 bullet points summarizing their situation
   - Tone: reflective, not prescriptive

3. **Current State Slide** — A visual representation of their current workflow/process with pain points highlighted
   - Process steps
   - Where friction exists
   - Estimated impact of each pain point (time, money, risk)

4. **Desired Future State Slide** — What success looks like for them
   - Their stated goals, reflected back
   - Quantified where possible

5. **Gap Analysis Slide** — The gap between current state and desired state
   - 3-4 specific gaps
   - For each gap: the cost of inaction

6. **Recommended Approach (Preview)** — High-level overview of how you'd address their needs (not a full proposal — a teaser)
   - 3 capability areas that map to their gaps
   - "In our next meeting, we'll show you exactly how..."

7. **Next Steps Slide** — Proposed timeline and meeting agenda for the solution presentation
   - Who should attend
   - What you'll cover
   - Specific date/time proposal

8. **Confirmation Slide** — "Did we get this right?" with explicit ask for feedback/corrections

Include speaker notes for each slide. Tone should be consultative, not pushy — this is about proving you listened.
```

### Prompt 3 — Multi-Stakeholder Needs Alignment Presentation

Use this when you've spoken with multiple people at the prospect's company and need to align everyone before proposing a solution.

```
You are an enterprise sales strategist. Create a stakeholder alignment presentation for a complex B2B sale.

My company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Stakeholders I've spoken to:
- [NAME, TITLE]: Key concern is [CONCERN]. Values [PRIORITY].
- [NAME, TITLE]: Key concern is [CONCERN]. Values [PRIORITY].
- [NAME, TITLE]: Key concern is [CONCERN]. Values [PRIORITY].
- [NAME, TITLE]: Key concern is [CONCERN]. Values [PRIORITY].

Common themes across stakeholders: [LIST SHARED PRIORITIES]
Conflicting priorities: [ANY DISAGREEMENTS OR DIFFERENT ANGLES]
Decision maker: [WHO HAS FINAL SAY]
Budget holder: [WHO CONTROLS THE BUDGET]
Champion: [WHO IS ADVOCATING FOR YOUR SOLUTION]

Generate a presentation that:

1. **Acknowledges All Perspectives** — A slide showing each stakeholder's key priority, demonstrating you've listened to everyone (without naming individuals if sensitive)

2. **Common Ground Slide** — The 3-4 priorities everyone agrees on, framed as organizational objectives

3. **Priority Matrix** — A visual framework showing all stated needs mapped by:
   - Impact (high/low)
   - Urgency (high/low)
   - Recommended prioritization order

4. **Unified Vision Slide** — A single narrative that ties all stakeholder needs into one compelling vision of the future state

5. **Success Metrics** — Proposed metrics that matter to each stakeholder group:
   - Executive metrics (ROI, revenue impact)
   - Operational metrics (efficiency, time saved)
   - User metrics (adoption, satisfaction)
   - Technical metrics (performance, uptime, security)

6. **Approach Preview** — How your solution addresses the top priorities, with a clear phasing recommendation

7. **Speaker Notes** — For each slide, include notes on how to navigate different personalities in the room and keep the conversation productive

This presentation should make everyone feel heard while building consensus toward a decision.
```

## Product & Solution Presentations

### Prompt 4 — Product Demo Presentation

Use this to create a structured product demo that tells a story instead of just showing features.

```
You are a product demo expert who trains sales teams at top SaaS companies. Create a demo presentation script.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it does: [2-3 SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Their key needs: [TOP 3 NEEDS FROM DISCOVERY]
Demo format: [LIVE DEMO / SCREENSHOTS / RECORDED VIDEO + SLIDES]
Demo duration: [15 MIN / 20 MIN / 30 MIN]
Features to highlight: [LIST 4-6 KEY FEATURES TO SHOW]
Features to skip: [FEATURES NOT RELEVANT TO THIS PROSPECT]

Generate a demo presentation script:

1. **Demo Opening (2 minutes)**
   - Recap their needs in 3 bullet points: "Based on our conversation, you need..."
   - Set expectations: "In the next [X] minutes, I'll show you exactly how [PRODUCT] handles each of these"
   - Frame: "I'll show you [PRODUCT] through the lens of a day in the life of [THEIR ROLE/TEAM]"

2. **Scenario Setup (2 minutes)**
   - Create a relatable scenario using their actual use case
   - "Imagine it's Monday morning and [SCENARIO RELEVANT TO THEIR BUSINESS]..."

3. **Feature Demonstrations (bulk of time)**
   For each of the [4-6] features:
   - **Setup**: Why this matters to them specifically (one sentence connecting to their stated need)
   - **Show**: What to click/demonstrate and what to say while showing it
   - **Impact**: The outcome or benefit, quantified if possible
   - **Check-in**: A question to ask them ("How does your team handle this today?")
   - **Transition**: Bridge to the next feature

4. **Integration/Workflow View (3 minutes)**
   - Show how features work together in a complete workflow
   - Emphasize time saved and errors eliminated

5. **"One More Thing" Moment (2 minutes)**
   - A feature or capability you haven't mentioned that will genuinely surprise them
   - Save something impressive for the end

6. **Demo Closing (3 minutes)**
   - Summary of what they saw mapped to their needs
   - Immediate reaction question: "What stood out to you?"
   - Next step proposal

Include detailed speaker notes with exact phrasing, timing markers, and fallback plans if features don't load correctly.
```

### Prompt 5 — Solution Architecture Presentation

Use this for technical sales presentations where you need to explain how your product fits into the prospect's technology environment.

```
You are a solutions architect who presents technical solutions to enterprise IT teams. Create a solution architecture presentation.

Your product: [PRODUCT NAME AND BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Their current tech stack: [LIST KNOWN TECHNOLOGIES THEY USE — e.g., Salesforce, AWS, SAP, Slack]
Integration requirements: [WHAT NEEDS TO CONNECT TO WHAT]
Technical concerns raised: [e.g., security, scalability, compliance, data migration]
Technical audience: [WHO WILL BE IN THE ROOM — e.g., CTO, VP Engineering, IT Security, DevOps]
Compliance requirements: [e.g., SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, FedRAMP]

Generate an 8-10 slide technical presentation:

1. **Architecture Overview Slide**
   - High-level description of your system architecture
   - Describe a diagram showing: data flow, key components, integration points
   - Keep it simple enough for a CTO but detailed enough for an engineer

2. **Integration Slide**
   - How your product connects to each of their existing tools
   - API overview (REST/GraphQL, webhooks, SDKs)
   - Pre-built connectors available
   - Estimated integration timeline for each connection

3. **Data Flow Slide**
   - Where data lives (yours, theirs, shared)
   - How data moves between systems
   - Latency and real-time vs. batch considerations

4. **Security & Compliance Slide**
   - Your security posture (encryption, access controls, audit logging)
   - Compliance certifications you hold
   - How you address each of their specific compliance requirements
   - Data residency and sovereignty considerations

5. **Scalability Slide**
   - Current capacity metrics
   - How the system scales (horizontal/vertical, auto-scaling)
   - Performance benchmarks relevant to their volume

6. **Implementation Plan Slide**
   - Phase 1: Setup and core integration (timeline, effort, resources needed from them)
   - Phase 2: Migration and testing (timeline, approach, rollback plan)
   - Phase 3: Go-live and optimization (support, monitoring, training)

7. **Support & SLA Slide**
   - Your support model
   - SLA commitments
   - Dedicated resources for their account
   - Escalation path

8. **Technical Risk Mitigation Slide**
   - Top 3 technical risks they might be worried about
   - Your mitigation strategy for each
   - References or case studies from similar deployments

9. **Next Steps Slide**
   - Proposed technical evaluation process (POC, sandbox, pilot)
   - What you need from them to proceed
   - Timeline to technical sign-off

Include speaker notes that translate technical details into business value for any non-technical stakeholders in the room.
```

### Prompt 6 — Competitive Displacement Presentation

Use this when the prospect is currently using a competitor's product and you need to make the case for switching.

```
You are a competitive sales strategist. Create a presentation for displacing a competitor at a prospect account.

Your product: [YOUR PRODUCT NAME]
Competitor they currently use: [COMPETITOR NAME]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Why they're considering a switch: [WHAT'S NOT WORKING WITH THE COMPETITOR — e.g., "too expensive," "lacks features," "poor support," "scaling issues"]
Your key advantages over this competitor: [LIST 3-5 ADVANTAGES]
Their potential concerns about switching: [WHAT MIGHT HOLD THEM BACK — e.g., "migration pain," "retraining staff," "sunk cost"]
Contract situation: [WHEN DOES THEIR CURRENT CONTRACT EXPIRE?]

Generate a 10-slide competitive displacement presentation:

1. **Opening — Respect the Status Quo** (do NOT trash the competitor)
   - Acknowledge what the competitor does well
   - Frame the conversation as "evolving needs" not "bad choice"
   - "As your business has grown, your needs have evolved..."

2. **Their Evolving Needs Slide**
   - 3-4 needs they have today that didn't exist when they chose the competitor
   - Frame as growth and maturity, not failure

3. **Capability Comparison Slide**
   - Side-by-side comparison on 6-8 criteria (choose criteria where you win)
   - Use neutral language: checkmarks, partial support, not supported
   - DO NOT use the competitor's logo without permission — use a generic label

4. **Total Cost of Ownership Slide**
   - TCO comparison over 3 years: current solution vs. yours
   - Include: licensing, implementation, training, maintenance, opportunity cost
   - Show the hidden costs of staying with the current solution

5. **Migration Approach Slide**
   - Step-by-step migration plan that minimizes risk
   - Parallel run period
   - Data migration approach
   - Timeline from decision to full deployment
   - Your support commitment during transition

6. **Risk Mitigation Slide**
   - Every concern they have about switching, addressed directly:
     - Migration risk → your proven migration process
     - Training burden → your onboarding program
     - Data loss → your migration guarantees
     - Downtime → your parallel run approach

7. **Success Story Slide**
   - A case study of a customer who switched from the same competitor
   - Before: what they dealt with
   - Switch: how it went (timeline, challenges addressed)
   - After: measurable results

8. **Quick Win Slide**
   - 3 improvements they'll see in the first 30 days
   - Specific, measurable outcomes

9. **Financial Bridge Slide**
   - If overlapping contracts are a concern: how you'll help (credits, extended trial, contract alignment)
   - ROI timeline showing when the switch pays for itself

10. **Decision Framework Slide**
    - An objective framework for evaluating the switch
    - Weight the criteria
    - Let the data speak

Speaker notes should be diplomatic throughout. Never make the prospect feel stupid for their current choice — make them feel smart for considering a change at the right time.
```

## ROI & Business Case Presentations

### Prompt 7 — ROI Justification Presentation

Use this to create a financial justification presentation that helps your champion get budget approval.

```
You are a financial analyst who helps sales teams build ROI presentations. Create a business case / ROI presentation for a prospect.

Your product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Annual cost of your product: [$ANNUAL COST — including implementation]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Their industry: [INDUSTRY]
Their company size: [NUMBER OF EMPLOYEES / REVENUE SIZE]
Use case: [WHAT THEY'LL USE YOUR PRODUCT FOR]
Current approach: [HOW THEY DO THIS TODAY — manual process, spreadsheets, competitor, etc.]

Value drivers (estimate what you know):
- Time savings: [e.g., "20 hours/week of manual work across the team"]
- Error reduction: [e.g., "5% error rate costing $X per error"]
- Revenue impact: [e.g., "faster sales cycles," "higher conversion rates"]
- Cost avoidance: [e.g., "eliminate need for 2 additional hires"]
- Risk reduction: [e.g., "compliance violations cost $X per incident"]

Generate an 8-slide ROI presentation:

1. **Title Slide** — "Business Case for [PRODUCT]: ROI Analysis for [PROSPECT]"

2. **Executive Summary Slide**
   - Total investment over 3 years
   - Total return over 3 years
   - Net ROI percentage
   - Payback period in months
   - One sentence: "For every $1 invested, [PROSPECT] can expect $[X] in return over 3 years."

3. **Investment Breakdown Slide**
   - Year 1, 2, 3 costs:
     - Software licensing
     - Implementation / onboarding
     - Training
     - Internal resource commitment (hours, not dollars unless known)
   - Total 3-year investment

4. **Value Driver #1 — Time Savings**
   - Hours saved per week/month
   - Fully loaded labor cost per hour (use industry average if unknown)
   - Annual value of time saved
   - Qualitative benefit: what people do with reclaimed time

5. **Value Driver #2 — Error Reduction / Quality Improvement**
   - Current error rate and cost per error
   - Expected reduction with your product
   - Annual value of errors avoided
   - Risk reduction value

6. **Value Driver #3 — Revenue / Growth Impact**
   - How your product impacts revenue-generating activities
   - Conservative estimate of revenue uplift
   - Show the math with clear assumptions

7. **3-Year ROI Model Slide**
   - Year-by-year table:
     | | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Total |
     | Investment | | | | |
     | Time savings | | | | |
     | Error reduction | | | | |
     | Revenue impact | | | | |
     | Net value | | | | |
     | Cumulative ROI | | | | |
   - Conservative, moderate, and aggressive scenarios

8. **Recommendation Slide**
   - Clear recommendation with confidence level
   - Key assumptions listed (so they can adjust)
   - Comparison: "Cost of doing nothing" vs. "Cost of implementing"
   - Next steps to proceed

Include speaker notes positioning this as a tool your champion can present to their CFO or budget committee. Every number should have a stated assumption so it doesn't feel made up.
```

### Prompt 8 — Cost of Inaction Presentation

Use this when a prospect is dragging their feet. This reframes the conversation from "cost of buying" to "cost of not buying."

```
You are a strategic sales advisor. Create a "cost of inaction" presentation for a prospect who is delaying a purchasing decision.

Your product: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it solves: [THE PROBLEM]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]
Their industry: [INDUSTRY]
How long they've been evaluating: [WEEKS/MONTHS]
Why they're delaying: [BUDGET FREEZE / COMPETING PRIORITIES / RISK AVERSION / UNCLEAR ROI / COMMITTEE DECISION]

Known pain points they have today:
- [PAIN POINT 1 — with any quantification available]
- [PAIN POINT 2]
- [PAIN POINT 3]

Generate a 6-slide "cost of inaction" presentation:

1. **Opening — The Hidden Cost of "Later"**
   - A compelling statistic or story about the cost of delayed decisions
   - Frame: "Every month of delay costs your organization approximately $[X]"
   - Tone: helpful, not pressuring

2. **Monthly Cost Breakdown**
   - For each pain point, calculate the monthly cost of not solving it:
     - Direct costs (wasted labor, errors, inefficiency)
     - Opportunity costs (revenue missed, growth delayed)
     - Risk costs (compliance exposure, competitive disadvantage)
   - Total monthly cost of inaction: $[X]

3. **Cumulative Impact Slide**
   - A visual showing costs accumulating over 3, 6, 12 months of delay
   - "Since we first met [X months ago], this problem has already cost an estimated $[X]"

4. **Competitive Risk Slide**
   - What competitors in their industry are doing right now
   - Market trends that are making the problem worse, not better
   - The widening gap between companies that act and those that wait

5. **Quick Start Proposal**
   - A lower-risk way to get started immediately:
     - Pilot program with limited scope
     - Phased implementation
     - Money-back guarantee or performance commitment
   - Remove the barriers that are causing delay

6. **Decision Framework Slide**
   - A simple 2x2: Cost of acting vs. cost of waiting
   - "The question isn't whether to solve this — it's whether another [TIME PERIOD] of this cost is acceptable"
   - Gentle but clear call to action

Speaker notes should never be aggressive or create artificial urgency. The goal is genuine insight into what inaction costs them, delivered as a helpful analysis.
```

## Pricing & Proposal Presentations

### Prompt 9 — Pricing Proposal Presentation

Use this to present your pricing in a structured way that anchors on value, not cost.

```
You are a pricing strategy consultant. Create a pricing proposal presentation that leads with value and presents pricing in the most favorable context.

Your product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Prospect: [PROSPECT COMPANY NAME]

Pricing options:
- Option 1 (Recommended): [PACKAGE NAME, PRICE, WHAT'S INCLUDED]
- Option 2: [PACKAGE NAME, PRICE, WHAT'S INCLUDED]
- Option 3: [PACKAGE NAME, PRICE, WHAT'S INCLUDED]

Implementation/onboarding cost: [ONE-TIME COST]
Contract terms: [MONTHLY / ANNUAL / MULTI-YEAR — ANY DISCOUNTS FOR LONGER TERMS]
Estimated value/ROI: [WHAT THE PROSPECT WILL GET IN RETURN]

Generate a 7-slide pricing presentation:

1. **Value Recap Slide** — Before showing any numbers, recap the value they'll receive:
   - Summary of their pain points
   - The outcomes your solution delivers
   - Quantified value where possible

2. **Solution Overview Slide** — What's included in the proposed solution:
   - Core capabilities they'll get
   - Implementation and onboarding
   - Support and success resources
   - Training included

3. **Investment Options Slide** — Present 3 options side by side:
   - Use "Investment" not "Price" or "Cost"
   - Highlight the recommended option visually
   - Feature comparison across tiers
   - Annual vs. monthly breakdowns

4. **Value-to-Investment Ratio Slide**
   - Total annual investment: $[X]
   - Estimated annual value: $[Y]
   - ROI: [Y/X]x return
   - Monthly cost reframed: "$[X] per user per day" or similar easy comparison

5. **What's Not Included (Upsell Tease)**
   - Additional capabilities available for future expansion
   - Framed as "room to grow" not "things you're missing"

6. **Terms and Timeline Slide**
   - Contract duration
   - Payment terms
   - Implementation timeline
   - Key milestones with dates

7. **Decision Slide**
   - Clear recommendation: "Based on your needs, we recommend Option [X]"
   - Specific next step with a date: "If we finalize by [DATE], we can go live by [DATE]"
   - Who needs to sign off

Speaker notes should guide how to present pricing confidently without apologizing for the cost or offering discounts prematurely.
```

### Prompt 10 — Executive-Level Proposal Presentation

Use this when presenting to C-suite executives who weren't part of the evaluation process and need the TL;DR.

```
You are an executive communications consultant. Create a 5-slide executive summary presentation for a C-suite audience who has 15 minutes and needs to make a decision.

Your company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
Prospect executive(s): [NAME(S), TITLE(S)]
What you're proposing: [SOLUTION OVERVIEW — 2 SENTENCES]
Investment: [$AMOUNT ANNUALLY]
Internal champion: [WHO HAS BEEN LEADING THE EVALUATION — NAME, TITLE]
What the champion has already approved: [WHAT'S BEEN AGREED AT LOWER LEVELS]
What needs executive sign-off: [WHAT SPECIFICALLY]
The executive cares about: [e.g., "revenue growth," "cost reduction," "risk mitigation," "competitive advantage"]

Generate a 5-slide executive presentation:

1. **Situation Slide** — One slide summarizing:
   - The business challenge (2 bullet points)
   - The cost of the current approach (one number)
   - What [CHAMPION NAME]'s team has evaluated (one sentence)

2. **Recommendation Slide** — One clear recommendation:
   - What you're recommending
   - Expected outcome (one sentence, quantified)
   - Investment amount
   - Timeline to value

3. **Evidence Slide** — Why this will work:
   - Results from a comparable company (1-2 sentences)
   - Key metrics from the evaluation/pilot if applicable
   - Risk mitigation in place

4. **Financial Summary Slide** — Numbers only:
   - Annual investment
   - Expected annual return
   - Payback period
   - 3-year net value

5. **Decision Slide** — What you need from them:
   - Specific approval needed
   - What happens next (immediate next step)
   - Deadline context (why now, without being pushy)

Speaker notes should be tight — executives hate wasted time. Each slide should take 2-3 minutes maximum. Include guidance on how to handle the executive who wants to challenge the recommendation (they will).
```

## Case Studies & Social Proof

### Prompt 11 — Customer Case Study Presentation

Use this to turn a customer success story into a compelling case study slide or mini-presentation.

```
You are a case study writer who creates compelling customer stories for sales presentations. Create a case study presentation.

Your product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Customer: [CUSTOMER COMPANY NAME — or "anonymized" if needed]
Customer's industry: [INDUSTRY]
Customer's size: [EMPLOYEE COUNT / REVENUE]
Customer's role (who you worked with): [TITLE OF KEY CONTACT]

The story:
- Before (the problem): [DESCRIBE WHAT THEY WERE DEALING WITH BEFORE YOUR PRODUCT]
- During (the implementation): [HOW THEY IMPLEMENTED — TIMELINE, CHALLENGES, SUPPORT]
- After (the results): [SPECIFIC, QUANTIFIED RESULTS THEY ACHIEVED]
- Timeline: [HOW LONG FROM IMPLEMENTATION TO RESULTS]
- Quote (if available): [ACTUAL CUSTOMER QUOTE — OR "GENERATE A REALISTIC ONE"]

Generate:

1. **Single-Slide Version** — A complete case study in one slide:
   - Headline with the key result
   - 3-column layout: Challenge | Solution | Results
   - Key metric in large font
   - Customer quote
   - Logo placement

2. **3-Slide Version** — An expanded case study:
   - Slide 1: The challenge (with context about the customer's business)
   - Slide 2: The solution (how they used your product, implementation details)
   - Slide 3: The results (metrics, quotes, before/after comparison)

3. **Verbal Story Script** — A 90-second spoken version for when you want to tell the story without slides:
   - Opens with context about the customer
   - Describes the pain point vividly
   - Explains the solution simply
   - Lands on the result with impact

4. **Proof Points** — 3-5 metrics from this case study formatted as standalone data points you can drop into any presentation

5. **Prospect Mapping** — Guidance on which types of prospects this case study resonates most with and how to customize the framing for different audiences

If the customer needs to be anonymized, generate appropriately vague descriptions that maintain credibility.
```

## Competitive & Comparison Presentations

### Prompt 13 — Competitive Comparison Presentation

Use this to create a fair, professional comparison between your product and competitors.

```
You are a competitive intelligence analyst. Create a competitive comparison presentation.

Your product: [YOUR PRODUCT NAME]
```

## Blog Post Featured Images

### 1. Conceptual Blog Header
**When to use:** Abstract or conceptual image representing a blog topic
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A visually striking blog header image representing the concept of "[TOPIC — e.g., productivity / personal growth / digital marketing / remote work]". An abstract, metaphorical scene: [VISUAL METAPHOR — e.g., a single illuminated lightbulb floating above a vast dark ocean / a person standing at a crossroads with diverging glowing paths / a tree growing from an open laptop with digital leaves]. Cinematic lighting with [LIGHTING — e.g., dramatic rim light / soft golden hour glow / cool blue moonlight]. The mood is [MOOD — e.g., contemplative, inspiring, energetic, mysterious]. Rich color palette: [COLORS]. Aspect ratio 16:9. No text, no watermarks. Editorial photography meets digital art.

**Pro tip:** Conceptual/metaphorical images outperform literal ones for blog headers. "Productivity" as a glowing compass is more compelling than a photo of a desk. Think in metaphors.

**Sizing:** 1200x630px (16:9) — works for blog headers, social sharing, and OG images

## Infographic-Style Images

### 6. Data Visualization / Statistic Image
**When to use:** Highlighting a key stat, data point, or research finding
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A clean, modern data visualization graphic highlighting the statistic: "[STAT — e.g., 73% of consumers prefer brands that personalize / Companies using AI grow 2.5x faster]". The number "[NUMBER — e.g., 73%]" is displayed in a massive, bold [COLOR] font as the dominant visual element. Below it, the context is written in smaller, clean sans-serif text. A subtle [CHART TYPE — e.g., circular progress ring / rising bar chart / upward trend line] in the background reinforces the data visually. Color palette: [COLORS]. Clean white or [BACKGROUND COLOR] background. Modern, editorial design quality. Aspect ratio 1:1 or 4:5.

**Pro tip:** Single-stat images are some of the most shared content on LinkedIn and Twitter. Make the number HUGE and the context small. The number should be readable at thumbnail size.

### 7. Process / Steps Infographic
**When to use:** Explaining a multi-step process or framework
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A vertical infographic showing a [NUMBER]-step process for "[PROCESS — e.g., launching a product / building a personal brand / creating a content strategy]". Each step is represented as a numbered node connected by a flowing line or arrows. Step 1: "[STEP 1]" with [ICON 1]. Step 2: "[STEP 2]" with [ICON 2]. Step 3: "[STEP 3]" with [ICON 3]. Step 4: "[STEP 4]" with [ICON 4]. Step 5: "[STEP 5]" with [ICON 5]. Clean, modern design on a [BACKGROUND — e.g., white / soft gradient] background. Color palette: [COLORS]. Each step has a brief one-line description. Flat design style with subtle shadows. Title at the top: "[TITLE]". Aspect ratio 2:3 (tall).

**Pro tip:** Keep each step to under 10 words. The infographic should be scannable in 5 seconds. If someone can't get the gist at a glance, simplify.

### 8. Comparison Infographic (A vs. B)
**When to use:** Comparing two approaches, tools, strategies, or concepts
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A clean comparison infographic showing "[OPTION A]" vs "[OPTION B]". Split vertically: left side in [COLOR A] and right side in [COLOR B]. Center: a "VS" badge. Under each option, [NUMBER — 4-6] comparison points listed with small icons: [POINT 1 — e.g., "Price: $99/mo" vs. "Price: $49/mo"], [POINT 2], [POINT 3], [POINT 4], [POINT 5]. At the bottom: a verdict or recommendation section with a subtle highlight on the recommended option. Clean, flat design style. Professional but scannable. Title at top: "[TITLE]". Aspect ratio 4:5.

**Pro tip:** Comparison content gets massive engagement because it helps readers make decisions. Be honest and balanced — one-sided comparisons lose credibility.

## Quote Cards & Text Overlays

### 11. Inspirational Quote Card
**When to use:** Sharing a motivational or thought-provoking quote
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> An elegant quote card with a [BACKGROUND — e.g., blurred sunset photograph with warm overlay / dark moody textured background / soft watercolor wash in pastel tones]. The quote "[QUOTE TEXT]" is displayed in [FONT STYLE — e.g., large elegant serif / clean modern sans-serif / mix of bold and light weight] font in [TEXT COLOR]. An oversized decorative opening quotation mark in [ACCENT COLOR] with 30% opacity sits behind the text. Attribution "— [AUTHOR]" below the quote in smaller text. The overall design feels [MOOD — e.g., refined and calm / bold and empowering / warm and uplifting]. Aspect ratio 1:1.

### 12. Bold Statement / Hot Take Card
**When to use:** Sharing a contrarian opinion or bold statement to spark engagement
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A bold, attention-grabbing statement card. [BACKGROUND — e.g., solid bright [COLOR] / dark black with a single accent stripe / dramatic gradient]. Large, oversized text reading "[STATEMENT — e.g., Most marketing advice is wrong. / Nobody cares about your logo. / Your competitors aren't who you think.]" in [TEXT STYLE — e.g., all-caps bold condensed sans-serif / mixed case with the key word highlighted in a different color]. The text fills most of the frame — it's designed to be readable at thumbnail size. No decorative elements, no images. Pure typographic impact. Small brand name or handle in the bottom corner. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Hot take cards drive comments (people love to agree or argue). The more specific and bold the statement, the more engagement. Vague platitudes get scrolled past.

### 14. Stat / Data Point Card
**When to use:** Highlighting a single powerful statistic
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

## Tutorial / How-To Step Images

### 16. Step-by-Step Tutorial Image
**When to use:** Showing a specific step in a process for a tutorial or guide
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A clear, instructional tutorial image showing Step [NUMBER] of [PROCESS — e.g., setting up a Shopify store / creating a Canva template / brewing pour-over coffee]. The image shows [SPECIFIC ACTION — e.g., a cursor clicking the "Add Product" button on a clean dashboard interface / hands placing a coffee filter into a dripper / a highlighted section of code in an editor]. Clean, well-lit, with [ANNOTATIONS — e.g., a red circle highlighting the key area / an arrow pointing to the important element]. The image is clear enough to follow without additional text. ### 17. Tool / Software Interface Preview
**When to use:** Showing what a tool or software looks like in a blog review

### 18. Before/After Edit Comparison
**When to use:** Showing the effect of a design, editing, or optimization change
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A side-by-side before-and-after comparison for [CONTEXT — e.g., a website redesign / a photo editing tutorial / a resume makeover]. Left side labeled "Before": [BEFORE — e.g., a cluttered, outdated website design with small fonts and poor hierarchy]. Right side labeled "After": [AFTER — e.g., a clean, modern website design with clear hierarchy, readable typography, and strategic whitespace]. Same content, dramatically different execution. **Pro tip:** Before/after comparisons are the gold standard for tutorial content. They answer "is this worth learning?" before the reader commits. Make the "after" genuinely impressive.

### 20. Workflow / Flowchart Visual
**When to use:** Showing a decision tree, process flow, or system architecture
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A clean, modern flowchart diagram for [PROCESS — e.g., a content creation workflow / a customer onboarding flow / a decision tree for choosing a tech stack]. ## Meme Templates for Marketing

### 21. Expectation vs. Reality Meme
**When to use:** Relatable, humorous content showing the gap between expectations and reality
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A two-panel "Expectation vs. Reality" meme image. Top panel labeled "Expectation:": [EXPECTATION — e.g., a person in a pristine home office with perfect lighting, a designer chair, two monitors, and a fresh green smoothie / a perfectly organized bullet journal with color-coded tabs]. Bottom panel labeled "Reality:": [REALITY — e.g., the same person in bed with a laptop balanced on pillows, surrounded by snack wrappers and a half-empty coffee mug / a messy notebook with scribbles and coffee stains]. Both panels are photorealistic. The contrast is humorous but relatable, not mean-spirited. Aspect ratio 4:5.

**Pro tip:** The best marketing memes target YOUR audience's specific pain points. "Expectation vs. Reality" works for any niche — just make the "reality" hilariously specific to your community.

### 22. Drake Meme Format (Brand-Safe Version)
**When to use:** The classic "rejecting one thing / approving another" meme format
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A brand-safe version of the two-panel approval meme format. Top panel: a professional [PERSON — e.g., businesswoman / entrepreneur] with a dismissive, "no thanks" hand gesture and slightly uncomfortable expression. Text beside them: "[REJECTED THING — e.g., Spending 4 hours on a social media post]". Bottom panel: the same person with a big smile, nodding approvingly with a thumbs up. Text beside them: "[APPROVED THING — e.g., Using AI to create 10 posts in 20 minutes]". Clean, simple background. The expressions are clear and exaggerated enough to read at small sizes. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Never use actual celebrities or public figures. Create your own version of popular meme formats using generic characters. It's safer legally and more on-brand.

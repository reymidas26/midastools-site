# 03 — E-Commerce Product Images

25+ prompts for product photography that sells — flat lays, lifestyle shots, mockups, and more.

## Product Photography — Flat Lay

### 1. Clean Flat Lay Arrangement
**When to use:** Organized product display from above for e-commerce or social
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A professionally styled flat lay photograph shot from directly overhead. [PRODUCT] centered as the hero item, surrounded by [NUMBER — 4-6] complementary props arranged with intentional negative space: [PROP 1 — e.g., fresh flowers], [PROP 2 — e.g., a textured fabric swatch], [PROP 3 — e.g., a small bowl of ingredients], [PROP 4 — e.g., a handwritten note card]. All items placed on a [SURFACE — e.g., white marble / light oak wood / sage green linen]. Soft, even overhead lighting with no harsh shadows. The arrangement follows a balanced, editorial grid composition. Photorealistic, product photography for a premium DTC brand. Aspect ratio 1:1. No text, no watermarks.

**Pro tip:** Flat lays work best with 5-8 items total (including the hero product). Use odd numbers of props. Leave at least 20% negative space.

---

### 2. Knolling / Organized Grid Flat Lay
**When to use:** Showing multiple items in a kit, set, or collection
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A knolling-style flat lay photograph showing [PRODUCT SET — e.g., a complete skincare routine / a travel essentials kit / a desk organizer set] with every item arranged in a precise, symmetrical grid pattern. Each item placed at perfect right angles to the others on a [SURFACE — e.g., matte gray / clean white / dark charcoal]. Items include: [ITEM 1], [ITEM 2], [ITEM 3], [ITEM 4], [ITEM 5], and [ITEM 6]. Equal spacing between all items. Overhead shot, perfectly perpendicular to the surface. Clean, bright, even studio lighting. Photorealistic, Apple product photography aesthetic. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Knolling (every item at 90-degree angles in a grid) is one of the most visually satisfying and shareable product photography styles. It signals organization and quality.

### 3. Ingredient Spread Flat Lay
**When to use:** Showcasing what goes into a food, beauty, or wellness product
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> An artful flat lay showing [PRODUCT] in the center, surrounded by its raw ingredients or components beautifully arranged. Ingredients: [INGREDIENT 1 — e.g., sliced fresh turmeric root], [INGREDIENT 2 — e.g., scattered black peppercorns], [INGREDIENT 3 — e.g., a small pile of ashwagandha powder], [INGREDIENT 4 — e.g., fresh ginger pieces], [INGREDIENT 5 — e.g., honey drizzle on a small spoon]. Everything placed on [SURFACE — e.g., raw stone / wooden cutting board / textured ceramic]. Natural, warm side lighting creating soft shadows. The ingredients look fresh, vibrant, and appetizing. Overhead shot, editorial food photography quality. Aspect ratio 4:5.

**Pro tip:** This "what's inside" style builds trust and transparency. Customers want to see real ingredients, not just a label. Use dewy, fresh-looking items.

## Product Photography — Lifestyle

### 4. Product-in-Use Lifestyle Shot
**When to use:** Showing a customer naturally using your product
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A warm lifestyle photograph of a [PERSON DESCRIPTION — e.g., woman in her early 30s with natural curly hair] [ACTION — e.g., applying / using / holding / pouring] [PRODUCT] in a [SETTING — e.g., bright modern bathroom / cozy kitchen in morning light / outdoor patio]. The moment feels candid and authentic, not posed. The product is clearly visible but integrated naturally into the scene. Warm golden-hour lighting streaming through a [LIGHT SOURCE — e.g., large window / skylight / open door]. Shot on an 85mm lens, f/2.8, with soft background bokeh. Earth-toned color palette. Lifestyle brand photography for a [BRAND TYPE — e.g., clean beauty / wellness / home goods] company. Aspect ratio 4:5.

**Pro tip:** "In-use" lifestyle shots convert better than studio shots for most DTC products. The customer mentally places themselves in the scene. Specify the exact action being performed.

## Product Photography — White Background

### 7. Clean White Background — Hero Shot
**When to use:** Main product image for an e-commerce listing (Amazon, Shopify, etc.)
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E), Midjourney

> A professional e-commerce product photograph of [PRODUCT] on a pure white seamless background. The product is positioned at a [ANGLE — e.g., three-quarter front angle / straight-on front view / slight 15-degree rotation]. Clean, even studio lighting from a large softbox overhead and fill lights on both sides, eliminating harsh shadows. A subtle, soft shadow beneath the product grounds it. The product fills approximately 80% of the frame. Every detail, texture, and material is crisp and clearly visible. Color-accurate, high-resolution commercial product photography. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Amazon requires pure white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds. Specify "pure white background, no off-white, no gray" for compliant images. This is also ideal for Shopify and any product listing page.

---

### 8. White Background — Multiple Angles
**When to use:** Showing the product from different perspectives in a single image
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A professional e-commerce product image showing [PRODUCT] from three angles, arranged horizontally on a pure white background. Left: front view. Center: three-quarter angle (the hero shot, slightly larger). Right: back or side view. Each angle is evenly lit with clean studio lighting. Consistent shadows beneath each product view. The product is color-accurate and every detail is sharp. Professional commercial photography for an online store listing. Aspect ratio 16:9.

**Pro tip:** Multi-angle shots reduce return rates by 25%. Customers who can see every side of a product before buying are more confident in their purchase.

---

### 9. White Background — Scale Reference
**When to use:** Showing the actual size of a product
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E), Midjourney

> A clean product photograph of [PRODUCT] on a white background, with a human hand [ACTION — e.g., holding it / placing it on a palm / gripping it for use] to demonstrate scale and size. The hand has [DESCRIPTION — e.g., clean, well-groomed nails, neutral skin tone]. The product and hand are in sharp focus. Soft studio lighting. The image clearly communicates the product's real-world dimensions. Photorealistic, e-commerce product photography quality. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Size reference images dramatically reduce "it was smaller than I expected" returns. Use a hand, a coin, or a familiar object for scale. Hands are most effective because they're universal.

## Product Packaging Mockups

### 10. Packaging Line-Up / Range Shot
**When to use:** Showing your full product line or collection
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A stunning product line photograph showing [NUMBER] variants of [PRODUCT] arranged in a [FORMATION — e.g., staggered diagonal line / gentle arc / V-formation]. Each variant in a different [DIFFERENTIATOR — e.g., color / flavor / scent]: [VARIANT 1], [VARIANT 2], [VARIANT 3], [VARIANT 4], [VARIANT 5]. **Pro tip:** Arrange products from lightest to darkest color (or smallest to largest) for a satisfying visual progression. Asymmetry often looks better than perfect symmetry in range shots.

## Product Comparison Images

### 13. Size Comparison Grid
**When to use:** Showing different sizes or variants side by side
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A clean product comparison photograph showing [NUMBER] sizes of [PRODUCT] arranged from smallest to largest, left to right, on a white background. Sizes: [SIZE 1 — e.g., travel/mini], [SIZE 2 — e.g., regular], [SIZE 3 — e.g., family/large]. Each product is labeled with its size or weight in small, clean text below. A thin dotted reference line connects the tops of each product to emphasize the size progression. Even studio lighting, consistent shadows. The image clearly communicates the size difference. E-commerce-ready, clean and informative. Aspect ratio 16:9.

**Pro tip:** Comparison shots reduce decision paralysis and customer service inquiries about "which size should I get?" Label each variant clearly with volume, weight, or dimensions.

---

### 14. Feature Comparison — Us vs. Them
**When to use:** Highlighting what makes your product superior
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A split-image product comparison. Left side (slightly muted colors, cooler tone): a generic, plain [GENERIC PRODUCT — e.g., basic plastic water bottle / plain white supplement bottle / standard office chair]. Right side (vibrant, warm, well-lit): [YOUR PRODUCT] with visible premium details like [DETAILS — e.g., brushed metal finish, embossed branding, premium materials]. The left side is labeled "[LABEL — e.g., GENERIC / STANDARD / BASIC]" and the right side is labeled "[LABEL — e.g., [BRAND NAME] / PREMIUM / OURS]". The visual quality difference is immediately obvious. Clean background. Professional studio lighting, better on the right side. Aspect ratio 16:9.

**Pro tip:** Don't bash competitors by name. Use generic labels like "Standard" vs. your brand name. The visual contrast speaks louder than words.

### 15. What's In The Box
**When to use:** Showing everything included with a purchase
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E), Midjourney

> A clean "what's in the box" product photograph. Every item included in the [PRODUCT] purchase is arranged on a white background in an organized layout. Items: [ITEM 1 — main product], [ITEM 2 — e.g., charging cable], [ITEM 3 — e.g., user manual], [ITEM 4 — e.g., carrying case], [ITEM 5 — e.g., extra tips/attachments], [ITEM 6 — e.g., warranty card]. Each item has a small, clean text label. The items are arranged with even spacing and right-angle alignment (knolling style). Overhead shot, bright even lighting. The image communicates thoroughness and value. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** "What's included" images significantly reduce returns and "I didn't know it came with..." customer service tickets. Be thorough — include EVERYTHING, even the manual.

## Before & After Product Images

### 16. Skincare / Beauty Before & After
**When to use:** Showing the results or transformation a product delivers
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E), Stable Diffusion

> A realistic before-and-after comparison for [PRODUCT TYPE — e.g., a skin serum / hair treatment / teeth whitening product]. Split image: Left side labeled "Before" showing [BEFORE — e.g., dull, uneven skin tone with visible texture under flat lighting]. Right side labeled "After" showing [AFTER — e.g., glowing, even skin with healthy radiance under the same flat lighting]. Same person, same angle, same distance, same neutral background. The only difference is the [RESULT — e.g., skin quality / hair shine / teeth brightness]. Clinical, honest photography — no filters on either side. Natural, diffused lighting. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Keep the lighting and angle IDENTICAL in both halves. Any inconsistency (better lighting on the "after" side) destroys credibility. Authentic before/afters build more trust than dramatic ones.

---

### 17. Home / Space Transformation
**When to use:** Products that transform a space (decor, furniture, organization)
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E), Midjourney

> A dramatic before-and-after room transformation. Left half labeled "Before": [BEFORE SCENE — e.g., a cluttered, disorganized closet with clothes falling off hangers, shoes piled on the floor, no system]. Right half labeled "After": the same space completely transformed with [PRODUCT — e.g., a modular closet organization system], showing [AFTER SCENE — e.g., color-coordinated clothing on matching hangers, shoes in clear boxes, labeled bins, everything has a place]. Same room, same camera angle, same perspective. The "before" has slightly cooler, flatter lighting; the "after" has warm, inviting lighting. Interior photography style. Aspect ratio 16:9.

**Pro tip:** Transformation images are the most shareable product images. The more dramatic the before/after gap, the more engagement. Make "before" look as chaotic as possible.

## Bonus E-Commerce Prompts

### 23. 360-Degree Product View Set
**When to use:** Creating multiple angles for a product page viewer
**Best for:** ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> A set of four product photographs of [PRODUCT] on a clean white background, showing four different angles: front (0 degrees), three-quarter right (45 degrees), side profile (90 degrees), and back (180 degrees). Each shot has identical studio lighting — large softbox from above-right, fill light from left, subtle backlight for edge definition. Consistent exposure and color temperature across all four angles. The product is centered in each frame at the same distance. Professional commercial product photography suitable for an e-commerce 360-degree product viewer. Each image at 1:1 aspect ratio. Arranged in a 2x2 grid.

**Pro tip:** Consistency across angles is critical. Specify "identical lighting setup, same distance, same background" to minimize post-production work.

### 24. Product Texture / Detail Close-Up
**When to use:** Highlighting material quality, craftsmanship, or fine details
**Best for:** Midjourney, ChatGPT (DALL-E)

> An extreme close-up macro photograph of [PRODUCT DETAIL — e.g., the stitching on a leather wallet / the grain of a wooden cutting board / the texture of a hand-thrown ceramic mug / the weave of a cashmere sweater]. Shot with a macro lens at f/2.8, shallow depth of field with only the closest detail in tack-sharp focus, the rest falling into a creamy bokeh. Dramatic side lighting raking across the surface to emphasize every texture, fiber, and imperfection. The image communicates craftsmanship, quality materials, and attention to detail. Rich, warm tones. Aspect ratio 1:1.

**Pro tip:** Detail shots build perceived value. Customers associate visible craftsmanship details with premium quality. These are especially powerful for handmade, artisan, and luxury products.

**When to use:** Dynamic, attention-grabbing product image

*Pro tip for all e-commerce prompts: Generate each product image in both 1:1 (product page) and 4:5 (social media) aspect ratios. You'll need both, and it's faster to generate them together than to crop later.*

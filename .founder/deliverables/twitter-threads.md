## Thread 5: The "ChatGPT Tips Nobody Tells You" Thread

**Target audience:** ChatGPT users (massive — "ChatGPT" gets 768M+ monthly searches)
**Hook angle:** Secret knowledge + immediate value
**Source post:** /blog/chatgpt-tips-tricks-2026

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
I've spent 1,000+ hours testing ChatGPT prompts.

Here are 10 tricks that 95% of users don't know — each one changed how I work:

**Tweet 2:**
1/ Tell ChatGPT to ask YOU questions first.

Instead of cramming context into your prompt, say:

"Before you start, ask me 10 questions about my business, audience, and goals. One at a time."

You'll get 10x better output because it surfaces details you forgot to mention.

**Tweet 3:**
2/ Use "Act as [Role]" but be SPECIFIC.

❌ "Act as a marketing expert"
✅ "Act as a senior B2B SaaS marketing strategist with 15 years of experience, specializing in product-led growth for companies at $1-10M ARR"

The specificity unlocks different knowledge.

**Tweet 4:**
3/ Chain prompts. Never ask everything at once.

Prompt 1: "Give me 10 angles for this topic"
Prompt 2: "Expand angle #3 into an outline"
Prompt 3: "Write section 1"
Prompt 4: "Now section 2, matching the tone"

Each step is 5x better than a single mega-prompt.

**Tweet 5:**
4/ Use the "Before and After" trick.

"Here's my current [email/bio/landing page]. Rewrite it to be 50% shorter, twice as clear, and end with one specific call-to-action."

This constraint-based prompting produces dramatically better results than "make this better."

**Tweet 6:**
5/ Ask for MULTIPLE versions.

"Give me 3 versions of this email:
- Version A: Formal and professional
- Version B: Casual and friendly
- Version C: Urgent and direct"

You'll always find a better option than if you asked for one.

**Tweet 7:**
6/ Use the "Format as" hack for instant structure.

"Format your response as a table with columns: Task, Time Without AI, Time With AI, Tools Needed"

ChatGPT follows formatting instructions extremely well. Tables, bullet lists, numbered steps — specify the format and the output is immediately more useful.

**Tweet 8:**
7/ Feed it examples of what you want.

"Here's an example of a product description I love: [PASTE]. Now write one for my product [DETAILS] in the same style."

Few-shot prompting works better than any instruction.

**Tweet 9:**
8/ Ask it to critique its own work.

After it gives you output, say:

"Now review what you just wrote. List 3 weaknesses and rewrite it to fix them."

Self-correction consistently improves quality by 30-40%.

## Thread 6: The "AI Email Hack" Thread

**Target audience:** Anyone who sends emails (everyone)
**Hook angle:** Practical + saves time today
**Source post:** /blog/ai-email-templates-2026

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
The average professional spends 28% of their workday on email.

That's 11.2 hours per week.

Here are 7 AI email templates that cut that time in half. Copy, paste, customize, send:

**Tweet 2:**
1/ THE COLD OUTREACH EMAIL

Subject: Quick question about [THEIR RECENT ACHIEVEMENT]

"Hi [NAME], Saw your [SPECIFIC THING] — impressive. I help [ROLE] do [OUTCOME] without [PAIN POINT]. Would a 15-min call this week make sense?"

Short. Specific. No pitch deck.

**Tweet 3:**
2/ THE FOLLOW-UP (after no response)

Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]

"Hi [NAME], I know things get buried. One quick thought: [SINGLE VALUE PROPOSITION IN ONE SENTENCE]. If the timing is off, no hard feelings. But if it's relevant, I'd love 15 minutes."

No guilt trips. No "just circling back."

**Tweet 4:**
3/ THE LATE PAYMENT NUDGE

Subject: Quick housekeeping — Invoice #[NUMBER]

"Hi [NAME], Hope all is well. I noticed Invoice #[NUMBER] ($[AMOUNT], due [DATE]) is still showing as unpaid. Likely just an oversight — could you check with your accounts team? Happy to resend if needed."

Professional. Zero awkwardness.

**Tweet 5:**
4/ THE TESTIMONIAL REQUEST

Subject: Would you be open to a quick quote?

"Hi [NAME], Working with you on [PROJECT] was a highlight of my quarter. Your results were impressive — [SPECIFIC METRIC].

Would you be open to sharing a 2-3 sentence quote about the experience? I can even draft something for you to edit."

The "draft for you" line doubles the response rate.

**Tweet 6:**
5/ THE MEETING REQUEST

Subject: 30 min this week? Re: [TOPIC]

"Hi [NAME], I need your input on [SPECIFIC TOPIC]. Specifically: [2-3 BULLET POINTS of what you'll discuss].

Could you do 30 min this [TUESDAY or THURSDAY]? I'll have an agenda ready."

Specific agenda = higher acceptance rate.

**Tweet 7:**
6/ THE PROJECT KICKOFF

"Hi [NAME], Excited to get started on [PROJECT]. Here's what to expect:

Timeline: [DATE] → [DATE]
Your first deliverable: [WHAT] by [WHEN]
Next step: [SPECIFIC ACTION]
My ask: [WHAT YOU NEED FROM THEM BY WHEN]

Any questions before we dive in?"

Sets expectations. Reduces scope creep.

**Tweet 8:**
7/ THE SCOPE CHANGE REQUEST

"Hi [NAME], Great news — [PROJECT] is going well. One thing has come up: [CHANGE NEEDED].

Impact: [TIME/COST CHANGE]
Options: A) [OPTION] or B) [OPTION]
My recommendation: [PICK ONE + WHY]

Happy to jump on a quick call if easier."

Never absorb scope creep silently.
